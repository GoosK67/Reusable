Approach
Cegeka ensures the successful creation of dedicated onsite teams through a structured, three-phase approach: Analysis and Design, Building the Team, and Ensured Staffing. This methodology guarantees a tailored solution aligned with the customer’s IT support requirements, operational goals, and culture.

Analysis and High-Level Team Design
The process begins with developing a high-level staffing proposal during the pre-sales phase. This proposal outlines the recommended for technical and governance profiles needed to meet the customer’s onsite IT support demands.
Cegeka combines industry best practices with customer-provided data — such as technical requirements, historical workload volumes, current staffing levels, and the number of office locations — to craft an optimized staffing plan tailored to the customer’s specific needs.

Building the Dedicated Team 
During the build phase of creating a tailored dedicated onsite team, Cegeka meticulously composes the team by pre-selecting profiles that align with the customer’s specific requirements. Beyond ensuring the necessary technical and generic skills for each role, we prioritize finding candidates who are an excellent match in terms of soft skills and personality, both with Cegeka’s culture and the customer’s environment.
To ensure alignment, our proposed candidate can be made available for an independent interview with a relevant customer representative. This step will provide valuable insights for the customer, ensuring full confidence in the selected team member.

Ensured Staffing with Skilled Backup
To ensure optimal staffing continuity within the dedicated customer team, Cegeka maintains a pool of highly skilled backup engineers who are specifically trained on the customer's environment. These engineers are ready to provide seamless operational support during both planned and unplanned absences, ensuring no disruption to service delivery.
To uphold the quality of knowledge transfer, we implement the concepts of shadowing and reverse-shadowing during the onboarding process. This approach facilitates comprehensive knowledge intake and ensures backup engineers are fully equipped to perform effectively.
The training duration is customized to reflect the complexity and specific requirements of the customer’s IT environment. It is typically defined in collaboration with the customer’s IT department during the transition phase and covers:
Secure Management of User Accounts: Proper handling of critical credentials and user access.
Domain Architecture and IT Infrastructure: A thorough understanding of the systems in place.
Procedural Standards: Familiarity with ticketing, registration, and security protocols.
Application and Systems Knowledge: Insight into the application landscape and operational systems.
Internal Workflows: Awareness of processes like software licensing and hardware procurement.

Overview of standard profiles
Onsite Service Desk Agent (OSDA) 
The Onsite Service Desk Agent serves as the single point of contact (SPOC) for end users, providing remote support through email or phone. Their primary goal is to resolve issues at the first point of contact, documenting all interactions in the ITSM system according to ITIL guidelines and a customer-defined priority matrix. In cases requiring further technical analysis, the agent escalates issues to the appropriate IT teams while maintaining responsibility for tracking and following up on tickets, ensuring regular updates are provided to users throughout the resolution process.
The Onsite Service Desk Agent (OSDA) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.
Responsibilities:
First-line support - Provide support for both software and hardware issues through email, phone, or chat (if supported by the ITSM tool).
Issue resolution - Conduct initial analysis and resolve issues when possible, and escalate more complex issues to second-line support.
Incident management - Prioritize incidents, inquiries, and change requests using a customer-defined priority matrix focused on business-critical applications and processes.
Ticket tracking - Remain responsible for tracking escalated tickets, and ensure regular updates to end users regarding the progress of incidents, requests, or change submissions.
User documentation - Develop and maintain documentation to help users perform basic IT-related tasks.
Standard requests - Execute routine tasks, such as assigning permissions in Active Directory or creating user accounts, following established procedures.

Skillset:
Customer Service - Strong customer-oriented and customer-friendly communication skills, and multilingual communication capabilities to meet local business needs.
Problem-solving - A solution-focused approach to problem-solving.
Technical Proficiency
Proficient understanding of ITIL principles,  competence in managing user accounts and permissions within Active Directory.
Expertise in troubleshooting Windows and Microsoft Office environments, specifically in relation to end-user support.


Onsite Workplace Engineer (OWE) 
The Onsite Workplace Engineer is responsible for managing incidents and requests that require physical, onsite intervention, providing direct support at the end-user’s workplace while also acting as the "hands-and-eyes" for remote IT teams. Operating with greater mobility than the Onsite Service Desk Agent, the OWE performs interventions across various buildings or locations as needed and may function at both L1 (e.g., interacting with users at a walk-in desk) and L2 levels, depending on IT processes. All incidents and requests are processed and tracked using the existing ITSM tool, in accordance with established procedures.
The Onsite Workplace Engineer (OWE) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.
Responsibilities:
Hands-On Support - Provide assistance to end users in office or production environments.
Walk-In Desk Assistance - Offer support at walk-in desks, serving as an additional contact point for workplace issues.
Meeting Room Support - Help users troubleshoot or connect to meeting systems in customer offices.
IMACD Requests - Execute and support IMACD (Install, Move, Add, Change & Disposal) requests and standard changes via established processes and order portals.
Migrations and Project Work - Assist with large-scale system updates or hardware migrations (e.g., transitioning from Windows 10 to Windows 11).
Onsite Support for Remote Teams - Serve as the onsite “hands-and-eyes” for remote IT teams managing offsite systems.
Stock Management - Manage stock levels for end-user hardware and other workplace equipment, following predefined customer procedures.

Skillset:
Customer Communication - Strong customer-oriented communication skills for a friendly and professional user experience, and proficient in multiple languages to meet local business needs.
Proactive Problem-Solving - Proactive approach with a strong sense of ownership for incident resolution and request management.
Technical Proficiency
Working knowledge of ITIL principles to ensure process alignment and efficiency. 
Competence in managing user accounts and permissions within Active Directory. 
Proficient in troubleshooting Windows, Microsoft Office, and other end-user software issues. 
Technical troubleshooting skills for end-user hardware, printers, and IT-controlled production components. 
Advisory capabilities regarding hardware and mobile devices (Android/iOS). 
Experience in deploying end-user hardware using SCCM, Intune, or other tools, following customer-specific procedures.

Additionally, the OTE is skilled in handling datacentre-related IMACD services, ensuring stability and efficiency in operations. Their deep technical expertise not only resolves advanced issues but also supports the ongoing enhancement and future-proofing of your IT environment.
The Onsite Technical Engineer (OTE) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.

Issue Resolution - Diagnose and resolve IT-related issues, minimizing downtime and disruptions.
Process Improvement - Propose and implement improvements to the IT environment, driving operational excellence, and support shift-to-left initiatives to streamline support processes and empower end users.
Professional Development - Stay updated on industry developments to maintain professional knowledge in the rapidly evolving IT landscape.

Proactive Mindset - Collaborate with customers to understand and effectively address their needs.
Analytical Skills - Ability to analyse and document the IT environment for better insights and management.

Proficiency in Active Directory configuration for effective user management.
Expertise in Windows Server, Azure, O365, SCCM, Intune, basic networking, and backup solutions.
Experience in scripting and automation to improve processes and efficiency.

The OSE is essential for resolving critical incidents, performing root cause analysis, and future-proofing systems through innovation and automation, ultimately ensuring the stability, efficiency, and continuous growth of the IT environment.
The Onsite System Engineer (OSE) is only available as an UNMANAGED staffing service.

Infrastructure Projects - Conduct critical research, offer recommendations, and develop infrastructure projects aimed at future-proofing current IT systems, with a significant emphasis on automation and process optimization.
Documentation - Document existing and new systems to facilitate knowledge transfer and support lower levels within the IT organization.

Glossary
Responsibility matrix
= Responsible: Party responsible for performing the activity and/or achieve the task. One party is Responsible, although other Parties can be delegated to assist in the actual work if required. An Accountable must sign off (approve) on work that Responsible provides. Responsible is by default proactive.
= Accountable: Party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by Responsible. Accountable is by default reactive.
= Consulted: Party to be consulted (whose opinions are sought in two-way communication) when performing tasks and/or activities. Consulted may also provide requirements on scope, result expectations, etc. Consulted is by default reactive.
= Informed: Party to be informed / kept up-to-date on tasks and/or activities (just one-way communication). Informed often only on completion of the task and/or activity. Informed no formal role in realization and therefore by default reactive.

Definition IMACD
In the context of IT workplace management, IMACD refers to the process of installing, moving, adding, changing, and disposing of IT assets. It is a critical function that enables organizations to maintain and optimize their IT infrastructure while ensuring business continuity.
Install: Installing new hardware in the customer environment, including new workstations, printers, and other end user workplace related devices.
Move: Moving IT assets from one location to another, whether it’s within the same building or between different sites.
Add: Adding new devices to customer environment, configuring workstations and other devices.
Change: Changing the configuration or settings of existing IT assets, such as upgrading hardware, updating software, or modifying system configurations.
Dispose: Disposing of IT assets that are no longer needed or have reached the end of their useful life, including retiring old hardware and securely wiping data from storage devices.
