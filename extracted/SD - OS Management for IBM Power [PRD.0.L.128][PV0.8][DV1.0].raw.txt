OS Management – iSeries OS
Service summary
This Service manages all aspects related to <klant>’s iSeries implementation hosted at Cegeka.
Standard service components
iSeries OS management
Assuring availability of the OS corresponding to <klant>’s Service Level;
Analysis of system logs, identification and resolving of issues;
Documentation of system configurations;
Performance tuning of the OS;
Active management of files and disk space;
License management of the OS and underlying components.
Patching & Lifecycle management
Implement interim patches issued via security alerts through Cegeka’s emergency change process;
Implement patches addressing specific issues for The <klant> (after agreement with the <klant>, via Cegeka’s emergency change process);
Each Quarter the Systems are fully patched.
This service doesn’t include:
The configuration and/or activation of additional features;
Necessary changes and project activities caused by patch management;
Software updates for which it’s necessary to change the application and/or reconfigure related systems with which the application interacts.
Backup & Restore management
This feature contains:
Backup and Restore of iSeries will be documented in the backup plan as part of the Document for Agreements and Procedures (DAP).
Management of the backup solution to a dedicated tape robot
Offsite storage of the tapes (via courier)
Management of the backup schemes
Restore on request
Physical iSeries Management
This feature contains:
Management of the hardware support contracts
Firmware patch management
Eligibility and Prerequisites
Conditions
The Cegeka managed services personnel needs to have network access to the Managed iSeries Server.
The Cegeka managed services personnel needs to have a login with full administrative access on the Managed iSeries Server.
Responsibility matrix

Monitoring & Reporting
This feature is focused on status and availability monitoring and includes:
Measurement of system parameters of <klant>’s iSeries OS;
Verification of availability of services, processes and ports of the <klant> applications. Detailed requirements need to be agreed upon separately with <klant>, and will be documented in the monitoring plan; 
Notification via e-mail and/or SMS when exceeding predefined thresholds and predefined statuses;
Visualization of monitoring results (events and trends) via a central monitoring portal.
The list of standard monitoring settings and the list of monitored system parameters are documented in a standard monitoring plan. This is used as a basis for a customized monitoring plan to fit the needs of <klant>. 
<klant> has an insight in the outcome of monitoring by means of monthly reports. An external monitoring portal can be provided as an option to <klant>.
