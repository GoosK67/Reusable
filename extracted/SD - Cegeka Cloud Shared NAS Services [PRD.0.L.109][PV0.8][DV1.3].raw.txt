Service Description
Service Summary
This section gives a brief summary of the described Service. 

The Service covers all tasks and activities required to deliver the Shared NAS Service. Through this managed service offering, Cegeka aims to relieve the Customer of the technical responsibilities associated with managing and operating complex NAS infrastructure.

The standard Service features are designed to ensure the platform supporting the Shared NAS Service remains operational, secure, and technically optimized.


Services
The Shared NAS Service provides a network-attached storage platform that delivers general-purpose file services within Cegeka's Cloud infrastructure. 

It operates on dedicated hardware that runs its own operating system and offers a range of integrated capabilities. The service includes the use of storage capacity as well as several key features, including MetroMirror, synchronous and asynchronous replication, enterprise WORM functionality, snapshot capabilities, antivirus and auditing integrations, and built-in ransomware protection. Together, these capabilities ensure a reliable, secure, and versatile file-storage environment for customer workloads.

Security and Compliance
Security and compliance are central to any Service delivered by Cegeka. Cegeka ensures that all infrastructure components are deployed, configured, and maintained in line with recognized security standards and agreed contractual requirements. The goal is to protect the infrastructure component and its data while aligning with both Cegeka’s internal security practices and the Customer’s specific policies.

For this particular Service, the supporting platform -based on NetApp technology- has been configured follwing NetApp's security hardening guidelines, which provides a set of controls allowing hardening of the ONTAP storage operating system.

Security incident management is performed in accordance with Cegeka’s internal policies as well as the contractual security requirements of the Customer. When a potential breach is detected, a security incident ticket is created and communicated without delay to both the Customer and the relevant Cegeka stakeholders.

Privileged access for Cegeka engineering teams, as well as privileged access for customers or third parties to the platform delivering the Shared NAS Service is carried out by Cegeka in close collaboration with the Customer. Account management follows the agreed Customer’s identity and access management (IAM) policies to ensure secure and compliant access practices.


Patch Management 
Effective patch management is critical for keeping systems up to date, minimizing the attack surface, and ensuring that the infrastructure, operating system, applications, etc... remain fully supported. This section outlines the patch policy governing the service and describes the specific approach applied to each component.

Regular patching is a fundamental element of the Service’s operational management. High-risk vulnerabilities requiring urgent remediation will be addressed outside the standard patching cycle through expedited updates.

Upgrades are performed only when recommended by the vendor, based on internal metrics such as run hours, version stability, and identified defects. The following sections describe the patching policy applicable to this service:

OS patching
Potential operating system upgrades for storage systems are evaluated on a quarterly basis. Upgrades are typically scheduled when at least one of the following conditions is met: an important security patch is required to mitigate a vulnerability; a critical stability update is needed to resolve functional issues or performance degradation; or a new feature, available only in a more recent OS version, is actively required.

In addition to this periodic review and planning process, the Storage team may also initiate an ad-hoc OS upgrade if specific threats or issues arise that impact the quality or reliability of the service delivery.
Firmware updates
Firmware updates for components such as drives, drive shelves, and switches are performed in conjunction with operating system upgrades. An exception is made only when an ad-hoc firmware update is deemed urgently necessary due to specific threats or issues identified that may affect the quality or stability of the service.
Application updates
Updates for dependent applications, such as the ONTAP Mediator, are performed alongside OS upgrades when recommended by the vendor. An exception applies only when an ad-hoc update is deemed urgently required due to specific threats or issues identified that could impact the quality or continuity of the service.

Optional Services
This section outlines the optional services which may be added to the list of standard services and -when applicable- will be priced in addition to the standard offering.


The following options can be enabled, yet require a technical review prior to activation:
Antivirus configuration: This feature is not enabled by default on the platform. Integration is possible through the customer’s third-party antivirus solution (a prerequisite), where file scanning is offloaded to that external service. Cegeka will monitor the configuration to ensure proper operation. 

Enterprise WORM configuration: This feature is not enabled by default on the platform. When enabled, it allows a NAS server to be configured with a defined retention period (eg. three years) during which file deletion is not permitted.

Autonomous Ransomware Protection: This feature is not enabled by default on the platform. When enabled, the ransomware protection engine for detection and response will automatically create immutable snapshots upon a suspicion activity. This feature activation also triggers the adoption of a prefdefined a immutable snapshot schedule. More technical details are documented in Chapter 6. Annex

Activation of any optional service feature requires the creation of an RFC, which will be handled in accordance with the standard change process.

Annex
Technical details
The following section provides more technical details about the underlying platform in support of the described Shared NAS Service. 

Autonomous Ransomware Protection
Autonomous Ransomware Protection (ARP) is a native ONTAP capability designed to provide secure, built-in data protection at the storage layer. It delivers real-time detection and response by monitoring file extensions, data entropy, and IOPS patterns. Through AI-driven analysis, it can also identify sophisticated ransomware techniques, such as header manipulation or partial file-level encryption, which are increasingly common in newer attack variants.

When ARP detects suspicious activity, it automatically creates an immutable snapshot. Such activities trigger alerts which -when integrated with Cegeka's Modern SOC- are forwarded our SOC analysts for further assessment.

All ARP-triggered snapshots are immutable (ref. 6.1.1.1) and cannot be removed, ensuring that the protected data remains unaltered and undeletable, even by administrators. This guarantees a dependable recovery point in the event of an actual attack.

The AI model powering ARP is pre-trained externally from ONTAP and is continuously updated by NetApp to maintain the effectiveness of its detection capabilities.


6.1.1.1 Immutable Snapshot Schedule
The immutable snapshot schedule serves as a proactive safeguard, enabling rapid recovery in situations where ransomware activity may have altered files without being detected by the ARP. Users can restore affected files directly through the 'previous versions'- feature, eliminating the need to perform a restore from backup.
