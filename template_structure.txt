119: [Heading 3] SLA & KPI Management 
120: [Normal] KPI metrics are defined in the service description. These metrics help set customer contract expectations about the service and are usually non-technical or lightly detailed. Service description includes high-level KPI’s: used for a broad audience and to communicate essential expectations.
121: [Normal] Within this document more details are addressed like: monitoring mechanisms, escalation paths, and penalties/rewards for breaches. Include operational-level SLAs tied to specific parts of the solution, such as cloud infrastructure response times, data recovery objectives, or API performance. Solution contains precise technical, operational, and contractual commitments making sure that IT teams, architects, and operational managers who need to understand the detailed commitments and how SLAs align with the technical design.
122: [Normal] Examples:
123: [Normal] Service description providing KPI definitions:
124: [List Paragraph] "This service guarantees 99.9% uptime per month."
125: [List Paragraph] "Critical support requests will receive a response within 2 hours."
126: [Normal] Presales guide contains SLA conditions:
127: [List Paragraph] "API latency will not exceed 200ms for 95% of requests, measured monthly."
128: [List Paragraph] "Data backup recovery point objective (RPO) is 4 hours, and recovery time objective (RTO) is 24 hours."
