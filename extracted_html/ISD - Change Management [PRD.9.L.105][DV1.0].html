The context of a process

The context of a process refers to the environment or circumstances in which a process operates. This includes all relevant factors that can influence the process, such as the goals, inputs, outputs, stakeholders, and constraints. Understanding the context of a process is crucial for designing, managing, and improving the process effectively.

A Procedure

A procedure is a set of established or prescribed methods to be followed routinely for the performance of designated operations or in designated situations. It is a series of steps or actions conducted in a certain order or manner to achieve a specific result. Procedures are essential for ensuring consistency, efficiency, and quality in various tasks and processes.

Context of a function

A function refers to the specific role or set of activities that a team or department is responsible for. This includes the tasks, responsibilities, and services that a team provides to support the organization's operations and objectives.

The context of a function refers to the environment or circumstances in which a function operates. This includes all relevant factors that can influence the function, such as the goals, inputs, outputs, stakeholders, and constraints. Understanding the context of a function is crucial for designing, managing, and improving the function effectively.

Cegeka Multi Compliance Framework (MCF)

The change management process is closely linked with the Cegeka Multi Compliance Framework (MCF). The framework provides a set of controls and procedures to ensure compliance with various regulatory and operational requirements. Each change type and step in the change management process must align with the MCF to ensure that all changes are compliant and properly documented.

By adhering to the defined procedures and aligning with frameworks such as the Cegeka Multi Compliance Framework, the organization can manage changes effectively while minimizing risks and ensuring service quality.

          1. Auditable Elements Primary Flow

Certain elements of the change management process are subject to audits to ensure compliance with the MCF. This includes the documentation of change requests, approvals, impact assessments, testing, and post-implementation reviews. The process must maintain a clear audit trail for all changes, providing evidence of compliance with the defined controls.

          1. Auditable Elements Emergency Changes

Emergency changes are handled through an exception procedure due to their urgent nature. These changes bypass the standard process, but certain controls must still be followed:

  • Record the change in the system within one working day.
  • Document the reason for the emergency change and obtain formal authorization from the customer via email.
  • Conduct testing or document the decision not to test the change.
  • Document preventive actions and conduct a post-evaluation at the CAB meeting.

Customer Contract Relationship

The service description of a product and the Cegeka Cross Functional Description is part of the customer contract:

  • In the service description of a product, changes (standard, optional and request services) included as part of the products, are listed and managed according to Cegeka's change management process and default timelines KPI’s and service levels. See WI Template Service Description – Chapter 2
  • The outlines of the change management process is includes the Cegeka Cross Functional Description document. See Product Portal Explorer (Public)
          1. Deviation and Ownership

If a product does not utilize the Cegeka Cross Functional Description, any deviations must be clearly documented and justified.

The ownership of the service description of a product lies with the product manager, ensuring that it is updated and maintained accurately. At present, the pre-sales team manages these descriptions.

The ownership of the cross-functional description lies with service design, but a dedicated owner is required to institutionalize and standardize the process.

Any deviations prior to agreement with customer must be confirmed with the Process Office.
