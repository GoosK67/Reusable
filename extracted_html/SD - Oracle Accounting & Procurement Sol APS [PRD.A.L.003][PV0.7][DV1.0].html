Service Description

Service identification

Service

Application Management for ‘Oracle Accounting & Procurement Solution - APS’ - Argenta

Unique ID

PRD.A.L.003

Service Model

Application Management Service (AMS).

This service is dependant of other layers (IaaS and/or PaaS) which are indicated in the ‘Obligatory’ chapter of this document and which have their own Service Description. The combination of the layers assures the customer of the ‘infrastructure up to the functional application’ management. These obligatorily services are called the ‘Platform’.

Service summary

Application Management Services

The Service provides the end-to-end management of the ‘Oracle Accounting & Procurement Solution - APS’ application (The Application) from a functional applicative perspective. Together with the underlaying obligatory Platform services, Cegeka unburdens the customer with all functional and technical aspects from infrastructure up to the functional Application.

The Standard Service features consist out of:

  • Functional Application Management service of the Application components by Cegeka Partner ‘RMC’
  • Technical Application Management service of the Application components by Cegeka Partner ‘RMC’
  • A service desk as single point of contact for the end-users of Argenta
  • Application development based on Argenta requests and specifications (T&M based)
  • Management of the underlying infrastructure by Cegeka Infra (as part of another Service Description)
  • End-to-end service management by Cegeka

The Application

The ‘Oracle Accounting & Procurement Solution (APS)’ provided and (functionally) managed by RMC.

Service features

Standard Service features

Application knowledge

The application knowledge resides at Cegeka’s partner ‘RMC’.

Application Management

The Oracle APS management Service provides end-to-end management for:

  • Oracle E-Business Suite (EBS)
  • Oracle Data Integrator (ODI)
  • Oracle Apex
  • Oracle Business Intelligence Enterprise Edition (OBIEE)
    • Oracle Data Integrator (ODI)
  • IVL (Finance “Informatie Voorzienings Laag”)

. This includes

  • Single point of contact service desk by Cegeka (see Cegeka infrastructure/workplace contract for details)
  • Technical Application Management of the Oracle applications by RMC.
  • Management of the underlying infrastructure (engineered systems and database) by Cegeka (see separate service descriptions on engineered systems and database management for details)
  • Applicative database management of ODI, EBS, OBIEE, IVL, APEX
  • End-to-end service management

Application Management

Functional Maintenance (by RMC) entails all non-infra and non-technical application issues and requests.

Technical Application Management

Technical Application Management (by RMC) includes the following tasks:

  • Oracle E-Business Suite
    • Deployment of custom development (packages, DB objects, …) on SIM, VAL, SIC and PRD.
    • Deployment on lower-level environments (DVL,ACC) is the responsibility of Argenta
    • Oracle EBS System Administration and Oracle Database Administration, including cloning, technical support, troubleshooting and investigating, performance issues
  • Oracle Data Integrator
    • Deployment of custom development (loadplans) on SIM, SIC, VAL and PRD
    • Access management
    • Connection management
    • Performing imports and exports
  • APEX
    • Deployment of custom development on SIM, VAL, SIC and PRD
    • Ensuring the availability of the application
    • Starting and stopping the application when requested by Argenta
  • OBIEE
    • Deployment of custom development (.bar / .rpd files) on SIM and PRD
    • Ensuring the availability of the application
    • Starting and stopping the application when requested by Argenta
  • Axway
    • Starting Axway MFT agents
    • Changes to the setup if requested by Argenta
  • Deployment of patches

Tactical Application Management meeting

Argenta, Cegeka and RMC hold each quarter a tactical meeting. Examples of topics to discuss include (but are not limited to):

  • Service performance review
  • Business case
  • Business forecast
  • Customer satisfaction
  • Follow up of the collaboration and relationship
  • Capacity management
  • Innovation initiatives

The meeting agenda will be defined up front based on questions and requirements of the three parties at that time.

Operational Application Management meeting

Argenta, Cegeka and RMC hold a two-weekly operational meeting, where the following topics are discussed:

  • Incident management
  • Request management
  • Problem management
  • Change management

Housekeeping

Recurrent maintenance activities (housekeeping) are needed to ensure the correct working of the Application. Automation is applied where possible.

Proactive management

Housekeeping includes following regular checks:

  • The running of the Application on its platform
  • Inspection of the Application after OS patching
  • system trends (trends of CPU/memory/disk usage) on deviations in evolution
  • assure the Application and Application settings are part of the backup of the underlaying platform

Resolution actions are triggered when needed and followed to assure the SLA.

Patching

The following patches are in scope of this Service:

  • EBS:
    • Security patches (2x per year), additional patches will be invoiced separately
    • Interim patches: ad hoc, including deploy when ready and urgent vulnerability patches
  • Oracle Data Integrator
    • One off patches
  • Apex
    • Security and other patches, when requested by Argenta
  • OBIEE
    • Security and other patches, when requested by Argenta
  • Databases
    • EBS database: within the EBS application patching
    • Other databases: according to the Service Description DBMS Management
  • Underlying systems
    • According to the Service Description Oracle Engineered Systems

Life Cycle Management

Cegeka monitors the Technology version of the service and notifies the Customer in accordance with the agreed LCM policy (see cross-functional Services) when used versions are subject to end-of-support or end-of-life. Appropriate actions (updates, replacement or other actions) are planned in accordance with agreed maintenance windows and will be executed on T&M basis in case of major updates. Patches and minor updates are included in the service cost as part of ‘Patching’.

Monitoring

Cegeka monitors the correct working of the Application.

In order to manage the environment properly, application monitoring is set up. This is done in Cegeka's monitoring platform, so that the monitoring of the applicationsis treated in the same way as the rest of the Argenta landscape.

  • EBS
    • Availability concurrent manager processes
    • Long running requests
    • Conc Requests in Warning / error
    • Invalid database objects
    • Mail queue of the notification mailer
    • Test pages webservices for “mandaten en domiciliëringen”
  • ODI
    • Availability of the ODI agent
  • OBIEE
    • Weblogic components
    • Availability of OBIEE analytics web page

The monitoring of the databases and underlying systems is described in the service descriptions for DBMS management and Engineered Systems.

Monitoring improvements will be done during the lifecycle of the Service and are based on evaluation of incidents, problem management or other suggestions.

The underlaying platform is also monitored according to the related Service Description of the platform.

Monitoring events are evaluated during the Service Window in accordance to the Service Level of the individual environment. Notable events are treated as Incident tickets and are handled according to regular incident management.

Security and Compliance

Management of users

User & Access Management for RMC accounts is included in this Service.

Detection of security incidents

The security management is performed following the Cegeka internal security policies and the contractual Customer security policies.

Security incidents are created in case a security breach is detected and is immediately communicated to the Customer and Cegeka stakeholders.

SIEM

Additional monitoring can be done based on System and/or Application logs, which can be forwarded to a Customer SIEM solution (Security reason) or other solution (compliance reason, e.g. central collection of application logs to oversee the application stack) on request.

Underlaying Platform hardening

The underlaying platform and OS will be hardened as part of and according to the Service Description of the related Services.

Backup and Restore

The operational Backup Plan contains the information on frequency and retention of the backups in accordance to the Customer backup policy.

Restores can be done both on file and on full system level. These can be requested via the regular RFC process.

Duration of a restore is dependant of the request (file or full; size of the item(s) to be restored).

Application settings are subject to the backup of the underlaying platform.

Incident and problem management

Besides monitoring incidents (see ‘Monitoring’) also other incidents can be reported by both the Customer and Cegeka. These will be subject to the regular incident management process (See Cross-functionals).

Related to incident management, Cegeka performs also problem management (See Cross-functionals).

Customers responsibility

The customer is responsible for the installation of the software during the release, based in the provided binaries and manual.

Requests

Both the Customer and Cegeka can issue a Request for Change (RFC) or a Request for Information (RFI).

There are multiple types of requests:

  • Service Request
    • This is a pre-defined request
    • Some can be requested via Horizon: these are pre-approved and pre-defined requests
    • Service request that is not pre-approved will be executed after approval by the CAB
    • Service Requests are executed as a change ticket and will be delivered within the indicated lead time.
    • The pre-defined execution is optimal automated.
    • This is used for requests that are often needed (multiple times per month).
    • These have a fixed price.
  • Normal Changes :
    • these are other non pre-defined requests
    • they need an evaluation and analysis where Cegeka needs to deliver a proposal and impact analysis to address the change request (with a T&M estimation).
    • A successive review, approval and planning (maintenance window) by a CAB of the proposal is needed prior to execution.
    • These are billed on T&M basis.

The handling of Requests is subject to the regular change management process (see Cross-functionals).

RACI role definition:

  • R-Responsible: The party who is responsible for the execution of the activity.
  • A- Accountable: The party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by the Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. The Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by the Responsible. The Accountable is by default reactive.
  • C-Consulted: The party who must be consulted and must approve or provide input prior to execution. This party (partly) gives direction to the result. This is about two-way communication.
  • I-Informed: That is the person/party who is informed about the decisions, progress and results achieved, so that the next step can be taken. This is about one-way communication.
