Service summary

Release and Deploy services

This service provides Argenta with the necessary assistance to support them in their release & deploy activities.

Whereas Argenta is responsible for the creation of the deploy scripts and testing them in the DVL environment, Cegeka is responsible for the execution of these tested scripts in the ACC, SIM, VAL & PRD environment.

All deployment is documented in tickets and are tracked within the Argenta ITSM tool, as such the Cegeka deploy engineers will need access to the tooling to be able to perform their jobs. In case database deployments are needed, the Cegeka deploy engineers will log a standard change on the Cegeka portal which will be routed towards the Cegeka database engineers.

The quality of the scripts and the outcome of the correct execution of these scripts is Argenta’s sole responsibility. In case of errors the Cegeka engineers will assist to the best of their knowledge (best effort).

Release activities will be coordinated by the Argenta Release Managers.

Service features

Standard Service features

SERVICE

DESCRIPTION

Major releases

Major releases are releases to production.

Major releases are executed during weekends.

Cegeka employees fulfill a planned and active role during a major release, the employees will stay online until Argenta release management announce the end of the release.

The following principles are applicable for these major releases:

  • The release calendar (quantity of major releases and high level timing) for the major release of the upcoming year will be communicated by Argenta by latest the 1st of october of the preceding year.
  • Cegeka ICT deploy & DBA resources will perform their activities remotely

Other Cegeka teams (like SQL, Unix, Wintel, NS, TAM, etc.) will be standby during the release weekend

The runbook of the specific major release needs to be communicated to Cegeka at latest the preceding Thursday end of business day, small updates can be done after this communication.

It is expected that Argenta performs its business tests directly following the deploy activities done by Cegeka.

Independent releases

Independent releases are releases without impact on de customers of Argenta, they can occur on production environments.

Independent releases are executed during weekends. The general release window for independent releases is on Sunday morning from 8:00 to 12:00

Cegeka employees will execute deploys and stay online or are standby until the tests are executed on the applications after that the employees stays standby and when needed plays active role for the independent release.

A maximum of 24 independent releases per year are foreseen (every other weekend). All Cegeka deploy activities for these independent releases will happen remote (meaning no onsite presence required) and the scope of work needs to be limited so it can be executed by 1 Cegeka deploy engineer and 1 Cegeka DBA. If needed

The timing of the release window and the estimated ‘size’ of the release needs to be communicated by latest on the preceding Monday.

The final runbook of the specific independent release needs to be communicated to Cegeka at latest the preceding Thursday end of business day.

It is expected that Argenta performs its business tests directly following the deploy activities done by Cegeka. If this is not the case and errors should come out of the test, the rerun will be done the next business day during the normal office hours.

Execution of ad-hoc tasks

Ad-hoc tasks are tasks not part of major or independent releases and can be requested by Argenta on an ad-hoc basis.

Ad-hoc task will be executed during Office Hours or outside Office Hours when planned and agreed with Cegeka.

Database deploys

Database deploys will follow a queueing system whereby requests before 16:00 will be executed the same business day.

Requests entering the queue after 16:00 will be executed the next business day or on best effort the same day (depending on availability).

Standby/On call

The Cegeka Release & Deploy service foresees, 1 deploy engineer in 24/7 standby and on call duty according to the following principles:

  • 24/7 standby for remote releases following a P1 incident;
  • After hours critical deploys to prevent a P1 incident or solve major impact;
  • Initiation can only be triggered by the Cegeka SDM after evaluation of the request.
  • In case of UCAB approved changes
  • When needed, the standby deploy engineer will contact the Cegeka standby DBA

Exceptional releases

An exceptional release is a major or independent release that is not communicated in time as defined.

In case Argenta wishes to perform additional release activities outside of the above possibilities, an exception can be requested to the Cegeka SDM based on the following:

  • A separate release planning needs to be requested at least 3 days upfront
  • Cegeka’s ability to fulfill these exceptional requests is based on best effort and remote presence
  • All exceptions will be charged to Argenta on a T&M via RFC base with a minimum of 2 hours per involved resource.

Reporting

Cegeka will use the report agreed with Argenta and based on the 4ME data to provide a reporting of the number of tasks on a monthly basis based .

This reported number of tasks will be used for monthly invoicing as stipulated in the Standard Service Billing.

Tasks

The total number of tasks for a month for the major releases, independent releases and ad-hoc requests will be determined like this:

  • Argenta delivers monthly, before the 10th calendar day of the next month, the number of tasks executed by Cegeka (based on 4Me information)
  • The invoicing is based on a fixed base amount of tasks (see Standard Service Billing) and and additional packages of tasks when the base amount is exceeded
  • (Note: Cegeka assures personnel availability for at least the base amount).

Requests

Both the Customer and Cegeka can issue a Request for Change (RFC) or a Request for Information (RFI).

There are multiple types of requests:

  • Service Request
    • This is a pre-defined request
    • Some can be requested via Horizon: these are pre-approved and pre-defined requests
    • Service request that is not pre-approved will be executed after approval by the CAB
    • Service Requests are executed as a change ticket and will be delivered within the indicated lead time.
    • The pre-defined execution is optimal automated.
    • This is used for requests that are often needed (multiple times per month).
    • These have a fixed price.
  • Normal Changes :
    • these are other non pre-defined requests
    • they need an evaluation and analysis where Cegeka needs to deliver a proposal and impact analysis to address the change request (with a T&M estimation).
    • A successive review, approval and planning (maintenance window) by a CAB of the proposal is needed prior to execution.
    • These are billed on T&M basis.

The handling of Requests is subject to the regular change management process (see Cross-functionals).

RACI role definition:

  • R-Responsible: The party who is responsible for the execution of the activity.
  • A- Accountable: The party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by the Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. The Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by the Responsible. The Accountable is by default reactive.
  • C-Consulted: The party who must be consulted and must approve or provide input prior to execution. This party (partly) gives direction to the result. This is about two-way communication.
  • I-Informed: That is the person/party who is informed about the decisions, progress and results achieved, so that the next step can be taken. This is about one-way communication.
