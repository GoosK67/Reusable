Security & Networking

Secure Mail

Service summary

For email servers that are hosted within the Cegeka datacenter, Cegeka can offer anti-spam and anti-virus measures to protect incoming and outgoing email. The service is based on Cisco IronPort

SPAM filtering is the process of determining whether a given mail is unsollicited and reacting accordingly by deleting, quarantining, tagging this mail. Virus scanning is running the files, attached to a mail through a virus scanner, and blocking infected mails, to prevent viruses from entering the network. Virus scanning uses standard available anti-virus software, which is embedded in the email security gateway.

SPAM filtering uses a number of different techniques to determine whether mails are legitimate.

  • Reputation filtering: each sending mail server’s ip address is given a reputation based on historical performance: the number of mails through this ip address, the amount of SPAM coming from this address, the number of blacklists this address is listed in, etc. This results in a number, the reputation score. Based on this score, different actions can be taken for mails (good reputation makes the mails pass, bad reputation blocks the mail. The Ironport Email Security appliances use the reputation service from Senderbase.org
  • Pattern matching: the appliances have an internal database of known words, phrases, that occur in SPAM messages. All mail is checked against this database, to determine whether they are SPAM or not.

The customer can choose to use the standard policy configuration that Cegeka manages or can request to have a dedicated and specific policy to match (more or less stringent) requirements a customer has.

Eligibility and Prerequisites

Conditions

  • The service may not be used for sending bulk (commercial) email. If a customer sends (or receives) bulk mailings that jeopardize the mail flow through the e-mail security gateway, the mail traffic of this customer will be throttled.
  • The customer must configure its servers or systems to adhere to SMTP guidelines and protocols when using the Cegeka service. When the e-mail traffic of a customer impedes the distribution of the e-mail traffic over all the available platform nodes, this customer’s e-mail traffic will be throttled. If Cegeka is responsible for managing the email server(s) of a customers, these demands are met by default.

The throttling is temporarily and only until the data flow traffic normalizes.
