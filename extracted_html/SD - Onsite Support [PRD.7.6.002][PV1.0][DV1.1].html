Introduction

A successful digital workplace is built on the ease with which users can engage with their digital tools. Through our User Enablement services, we provide everything our customer needs to empower their people to work comfortably and efficiently. Clients can rely on us for hardware, software, and comprehensive support.

Cegeka Managed Onsite Support Services (MOSS) provides a staffing solution tailored to technical and coordination roles, with a strong focus on supporting end users and their workplace devices at customer locations. Our adaptable staffing levels ensure we can meet changing business needs, supported by a team of customer-trained backup engineers to maintain consistent service delivery.

Added Value

Ensured Staffing

Our Managed Onsite Support Services prioritize operational stability and continuity by ensuring backup coverage is in place within one business day. This proactive approach ensures consistent support coverage across the service period, in line with SLA requirements, and provides a high standard of continuity and operational reliability. This approach allows your business to sustain optimal operational efficiency without the risk of unplanned staffing gaps or service interruptions.

Cost-Effective Support

We optimize staffing levels based on the size, number of locations, and dynamic delivery needs of our customers. By aligning our services with specific operational requirements, we provide cost-effective solutions that maximize both resource efficiency and service value. As integral part of the digital workplace, MOSS constantly seeks for efficiencies across Cegeka teams to ensure top-tier and cost-effective support.

Adaptable Resource Allocation

Designed to meet the fluid needs of your business, our service provides on-demand access to skilled backup engineers. This scalable model adjusts seamlessly for seasonal workload increases or temporary operational changes, allowing you to sustain the right level of support without compromising service quality.

Expertise and Certification

Our Managed Onsite Support Engineers bring the technical knowledge and targeted certifications required for end-user support, workplace devices, and peripherals. This expertise not only complements your existing IT team but also fills any knowledge gaps within your organization, offering immediate business value through specialized support.

Enhanced End-User Satisfaction

A dedicated onsite team, supported by skilled backup engineers, ensures end users receive consistent, efficient support from familiar professionals who quickly resolve issues. This approach enhances service continuity and user satisfaction by minimizing disruptions. Engineers are fluent in both the local language and English, ensuring seamless communication and collaboration across your organization, including remote IT teams, for smoother operational alignment.

Approach

Cegeka ensures the successful creation of dedicated onsite teams through a structured, three-phase approach: Analysis and Design, Building the Team, and Ensured Staffing. This methodology guarantees a tailored solution aligned with the customer’s IT support requirements, operational goals, and culture.

Analysis and High-Level Team Design

The process begins with developing a high-level staffing proposal during the pre-sales phase. This proposal outlines the recommended for technical and governance profiles needed to meet the customer’s onsite IT support demands.

Cegeka combines industry best practices with customer-provided data — such as technical requirements, historical workload volumes, current staffing levels, and the number of office locations — to craft an optimized staffing plan tailored to the customer’s specific needs.

Building the Dedicated Team

During the build phase of creating a tailored dedicated onsite team, Cegeka meticulously composes the team by pre-selecting profiles that align with the customer’s specific requirements. Beyond ensuring the necessary technical and generic skills for each role, we prioritize finding candidates who are an excellent match in terms of soft skills and personality, both with Cegeka’s culture and the customer’s environment.

To ensure alignment, our proposed candidate can be made available for an independent interview with a relevant customer representative. This step will provide valuable insights for the customer, ensuring full confidence in the selected team member.

Ensured Staffing with Skilled Backup

To ensure optimal staffing continuity within the dedicated customer team, Cegeka maintains a pool of highly skilled backup engineers who are specifically trained on the customer's environment. These engineers are ready to provide seamless operational support during both planned and unplanned absences, ensuring no disruption to service delivery.

To uphold the quality of knowledge transfer, we implement the concepts of shadowing and reverse-shadowing during the onboarding process. This approach facilitates comprehensive knowledge intake and ensures backup engineers are fully equipped to perform effectively.

The training duration is customized to reflect the complexity and specific requirements of the customer’s IT environment. It is typically defined in collaboration with the customer’s IT department during the transition phase and covers:

  • Secure Management of User Accounts: Proper handling of critical credentials and user access.
  • Domain Architecture and IT Infrastructure: A thorough understanding of the systems in place.
  • Procedural Standards: Familiarity with ticketing, registration, and security protocols.
  • Application and Systems Knowledge: Insight into the application landscape and operational systems.
  • Internal Workflows: Awareness of processes like software licensing and hardware procurement.

Standard Services

Staffing Services

Managed Staffing

We provide managed staffing services based on a full-time equivalent (FTE) model, prioritizing operational stability and continuity by ensuring backup coverage is in place within one business day. This service covers key technical engineering profiles, with skilled backup engineers available to ensure continuous support for each role within the dedicated customer team.

Non-Managed Staffing

All technical onsite profiles outlined in the standard services can also be onboarded under a non-managed staffing model. In this model, continuity during planned or unplanned absences will be restored upon the return of the primary resource. If necessary, an earlier replacement may be provided on a best-effort basis by an engineer without prior customer-specific training and without a defined SLA.

Onsite Service Desk Agent (OSDA)

The Onsite Service Desk Agent serves as the single point of contact (SPOC) for end users, providing remote support through email or phone. Their primary goal is to resolve issues at the first point of contact, documenting all interactions in the ITSM system according to ITIL guidelines and a customer-defined priority matrix. In cases requiring further technical analysis, the agent escalates issues to the appropriate IT teams while maintaining responsibility for tracking and following up on tickets, ensuring regular updates are provided to users throughout the resolution process.

The Onsite Service Desk Agent (OSDA) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.

Responsibilities:

  • First-line support - Provide support for both software and hardware issues through email, phone, or chat (if supported by the ITSM tool).
  • Issue resolution - Conduct initial analysis and resolve issues when possible, and escalate more complex issues to second-line support.
  • Incident management - Prioritize incidents, inquiries, and change requests using a customer-defined priority matrix focused on business-critical applications and processes.
  • Ticket tracking - Remain responsible for tracking escalated tickets, and ensure regular updates to end users regarding the progress of incidents, requests, or change submissions.
  • User documentation - Develop and maintain documentation to help users perform basic IT-related tasks.
  • Standard requests - Execute routine tasks, such as assigning permissions in Active Directory or creating user accounts, following established procedures.

Skillset:

  • Customer Service - Strong customer-oriented and customer-friendly communication skills, and multilingual communication capabilities to meet local business needs.
  • Problem-solving - A solution-focused approach to problem-solving.
  • Technical Proficiency
  • Proficient understanding of ITIL principles, competence in managing user accounts and permissions within Active Directory.
  • Expertise in troubleshooting Windows and Microsoft Office environments, specifically in relation to end-user support.

Onsite Workplace Engineer (OWE)

The Onsite Workplace Engineer is responsible for managing incidents and requests that require physical, onsite intervention, providing direct support at the end-user’s workplace while also acting as the "hands-and-eyes" for remote IT teams. Operating with greater mobility than the Onsite Service Desk Agent, the OWE performs interventions across various buildings or locations as needed and may function at both L1 (e.g., interacting with users at a walk-in desk) and L2 levels, depending on IT processes. All incidents and requests are processed and tracked using the existing ITSM tool, in accordance with established procedures.

The Onsite Workplace Engineer (OWE) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.

Responsibilities:

  • Hands-On Support - Provide assistance to end users in office or production environments.
  • Walk-In Desk Assistance - Offer support at walk-in desks, serving as an additional contact point for workplace issues.
  • Meeting Room Support - Help users troubleshoot or connect to meeting systems in customer offices.
  • IMACD Requests - Execute and support IMACD (Install, Move, Add, Change & Disposal) requests and standard changes via established processes and order portals.
  • Migrations and Project Work - Assist with large-scale system updates or hardware migrations (e.g., transitioning from Windows 10 to Windows 11).
  • Onsite Support for Remote Teams - Serve as the onsite “hands-and-eyes” for remote IT teams managing offsite systems.
  • Stock Management - Manage stock levels for end-user hardware and other workplace equipment, following predefined customer procedures.

Skillset:

  • Customer Communication - Strong customer-oriented communication skills for a friendly and professional user experience, and proficient in multiple languages to meet local business needs.
  • Proactive Problem-Solving - Proactive approach with a strong sense of ownership for incident resolution and request management.
  • Technical Proficiency
  • Working knowledge of ITIL principles to ensure process alignment and efficiency.
  • Competence in managing user accounts and permissions within Active Directory.
  • Proficient in troubleshooting Windows, Microsoft Office, and other end-user software issues.
  • Technical troubleshooting skills for end-user hardware, printers, and IT-controlled production components.
  • Advisory capabilities regarding hardware and mobile devices (Android/iOS).
  • Experience in deploying end-user hardware using SCCM, Intune, or other tools, following customer-specific procedures.

Onsite Technical Engineer (OTE)

The Onsite Technical Engineer is a crucial resource for maintaining and optimizing IT infrastructure, specializing in resolving complex incidents and implementing change requests at Level 3 (L3). Operating either from Cegeka or directly at your location, the OTE focuses on improving the workplace environment through advanced technical interventions such as scripting, automation, and shift-to-left strategies. Additionally, the OTE is skilled in handling datacentre-related IMACD services, ensuring stability and efficiency in operations. Their deep technical expertise not only resolves advanced issues but also supports the ongoing enhancement and future-proofing of your IT environment.

The Onsite Technical Engineer (OTE) is available through both MANAGED and UNMANAGED staffing services.

Responsibilities:

  • Infrastructure Management - Install and configure new IT server infrastructure and desktop platforms to meet business requirements, Integrate new peripherals into the existing IT landscape to enhance functionality, and Facilitate the relocation of IT infrastructure equipment as necessary.
  • Ongoing Support - Provide ongoing support and management for server environments to ensure optimal performance, and offer back-end support for IT managers to enhance operational efficiency.
  • Issue Resolution - Diagnose and resolve IT-related issues, minimizing downtime and disruptions.
  • Process Improvement - Propose and implement improvements to the IT environment, driving operational excellence, and support shift-to-left initiatives to streamline support processes and empower end users.
  • Professional Development - Stay updated on industry developments to maintain professional knowledge in the rapidly evolving IT landscape.

Skillset:

  • Customer Communication - Strong customer-oriented communication skills to ensure a positive user experience.
  • Proactive Mindset - Collaborate with customers to understand and effectively address their needs.
  • Analytical Skills - Ability to analyse and document the IT environment for better insights and management.
  • Technical Proficiency
  • In-depth knowledge of ITIL principles to enhance service delivery.
  • Proficiency in Active Directory configuration for effective user management.
  • Expertise in Windows Server, Azure, O365, SCCM, Intune, basic networking, and backup solutions.
  • Experience in scripting and automation to improve processes and efficiency.

Onsite System Engineer (OSE)

The Onsite System Engineer brings a high level of technical expertise, focusing on the broader IT infrastructure beyond workplace management. While the Onsite Technical Engineer specializes in maintaining and enhancing the workplace environment, the OSE addresses more complex, system-wide challenges by analysing, developing, and implementing advanced projects related to data centres, virtual environments, and server farms. This role aligns business requirements with long-term technical solutions, driving strategic improvements across the infrastructure. The OSE is essential for resolving critical incidents, performing root cause analysis, and future-proofing systems through innovation and automation, ultimately ensuring the stability, efficiency, and continuous growth of the IT environment.

The Onsite System Engineer (OSE) is only available as an UNMANAGED staffing service.

Responsibilities:

  • Third-Line Support - Provide third-line support and conduct root cause analysis for complex disruptions with a clear business impact, necessitating structural solutions.
  • Data Center Management - Manage data centre operations and existing (virtual) server farms, focusing on high availability and targeted patch management to minimize risks.
  • System Analysis and Configuration - Analyse, configure, and test new IT systems to adapt to the evolving needs of the business.
  • Infrastructure Projects - Conduct critical research, offer recommendations, and develop infrastructure projects aimed at future-proofing current IT systems, with a significant emphasis on automation and process optimization.
  • Documentation - Document existing and new systems to facilitate knowledge transfer and support lower levels within the IT organization.

Skillset:

  • Technical Communication - Proficient in technical communication with IT management and business line owners.
  • Senior-Level Profile - Senior-level professional with a proven track record in IT infrastructure.
  • Proactive Improvement - Demonstrates proactivity and ownership in enhancing efficiency within IT systems.
  • Innovation Focus - Strong focus on innovation and ongoing professional development through certifications and acquiring additional expertise.
  • Technical Expertise
  • Extensive expertise across a wide range of (virtual) technologies.
  • Experienced in configuring data centre-related backend systems and infrastructure

Onsite Team Lead (OTL)

The Onsite Team Lead plays a pivotal role in managing and optimizing the operational efficiency of the onsite support team within the customer’s environment, ensuring that internal IT services are effectively supported and day-to-day operations run smoothly. This individual combines technical expertise with strong leadership skills to oversee the onsite team and serves as the primary liaison between the customer and Cegeka. The Onsite Team Lead fosters a collaborative atmosphere that enhances service delivery and promotes continuous improvement in the IT landscape while overseeing daily resource planning, providing team development through feedback and coaching, and offering insights on projects and operational enhancements. Additionally, they coordinate incidents, changes, and problem tickets, maintain appropriate stock levels of end-user hardware, and deliver timely reports on Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Experience Level Agreements (XLAs).

The Onsite Team Lead (OTL) is only available as an UNMANAGED staffing service.

Responsibilities:

  • Team Management - Oversee general coordination and daily resource planning of the onsite team, and prioritize tasks to ensure high-quality solutions are consistently delivered.
  • Team Development - Provide management and development for team members through constructive feedback and coaching, and foster a culture of growth and excellence among engineers.
  • Customer Liaison - Serve as a trusted technical sparring partner for the customer, offering insights and guidance on upcoming projects, and propose enhancements to the IT environment and recommend measures for improved operational efficiency.
  • Incident and Change Coordination - Coordinate incidents, changes, and problem tickets, ensuring thorough quality control, accurate queue tracking, and comprehensive reporting.
  • Resource Management - Maintain appropriate stock levels of end-user hardware for centralized and remote locations, and proactively address low stock situations by initiating orders and coordinating logistics.
  • Reporting - Deliver transparent and timely reports on Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Experience Level Agreements (XLAs), and develop reports in consultation with the customer to meet their specific needs.

Skillset:

  • Customer Communication - Strong customer-oriented and friendly communication skills.
  • Proactive Collaboration - Proactive approach with a strong ability to collaborate and think strategically with the customer.
  • Critical Mindset - Focused on evaluating existing processes and structures, continually identifying and pursuing optimization opportunities.
  • Technical Knowledge:
  • Advanced knowledge of ITIL principles and practical implementation experience.
  • Senior-level expertise in service desk and end-user support services.

Scheduled Site Visits

Cegeka offers flexible scheduling for onsite visits with technical experts. During these visits, a designated engineer will complete necessary tasks and provide a detailed report on the work performed and any pending issues. This allows our customer to focus on core business operations, knowing that critical IT tasks are being managed. Common activities during these visits include routine checks of essential IT systems and updates to management tools. Only Onsite Workplace Engineers (OWE) and Onsite Technical Engineers (OTE) handle these tasks, ensuring the work is carried out by professionals with the appropriate expertise.

Ad Hoc Support Services

Cegeka maintains a team of skilled Onsite Workplace Engineers, readily available to address unplanned disruptions within our customer’s IT workplace environment. These engineers provide on-site, hands-on support to resolve technical issues swiftly, either by directly assisting end users or by collaborating with remote IT teams to restore business operations with minimal downtime. Access to this standby service is secured through a retainer fee, adjusted according to the customer’s geographical location. Two response options are available: same-day support with an engineer on-site within four hours, available 24/7, or guaranteed next-business-day support. The specific Service Level Agreement (SLA) will be tailored based on the location, ensuring prompt and reliable service delivery.

Conditions

  • Parking fees are at the customer’s expense.
  • The customer will provide needed (physical) access for all customer locations where Cegeka needs to provide the onsite services for all specified customer locations.
  • The customer will provide correctly configured customer accesses and accounts for all Cegeka engineers that provide onsite services for all specified customer locations.
  • The customer will provide access to all customer documentation and processes needed to provide the onsite services for all specified customer locations.
  • The “Non-customer trained” type of engineer has general IT knowledge and troubleshooting skills at the desired level, as described per engineering profile. These profiles act as smart hands for projects with bulk, IT related tasks such as bulk rollout of new end user IT equipment. If specific training and/or accounts are required by the customer, these can be handled as stipulated in the Responsibility matrix.
  • The service “Scheduled Site Visit” will only be performed by the technical profiles OWE and OTE.
  • For IMACD activities, all structural connection points need to be in place (electrical and network outlets, physical mounts for hardware on walls, ceilings, …). Cegeka personnel is not qualified or equipped to carry out those type of structural activities (drilling, electrical works, …).
  • For IMACD activities, all processes are defined, documented and readily available for the specific customer environment, including tooling that is needed to process these requests.

Prerequisites

  • Staffing levels and team composition have been determined in pre-sales stage
  • Customer specific training/onboarding have been given to all engineers in scope
  • Specific policies and/or procedures regarding security, privacy and data protection need to be communicated and documented by the customer or responsible Cegeka team.

Out of scope

Cegeka MOSS Management will not handle any customer operational matters or coordination. Responsibilities towards operational governance are stipulated in the corresponding Responsibility matrix. Everything that is not included in this service description is by default out of scope.

Responsibility matrix

= Responsible: Party responsible for performing the activity and/or achieve the task. One party is Responsible, although other Parties can be delegated to assist in the actual work if required. An Accountable must sign off (approve) on work that Responsible provides. Responsible is by default proactive.

= Accountable: Party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by Responsible. Accountable is by default reactive.

= Consulted: Party to be consulted (whose opinions are sought in two-way communication) when performing tasks and/or activities. Consulted may also provide requirements on scope, result expectations, etc. Consulted is by default reactive.

= Informed: Party to be informed / kept up-to-date on tasks and/or activities (just one-way communication). Informed often only on completion of the task and/or activity. Informed no formal role in realization and therefore by default reactive.

Definition IMACD

In the context of IT workplace management, IMACD refers to the process of installing, moving, adding, changing, and disposing of IT assets. It is a critical function that enables organizations to maintain and optimize their IT infrastructure while ensuring business continuity.

  • Install: Installing new hardware in the customer environment, including new workstations, printers, and other end user workplace related devices.
  • Move: Moving IT assets from one location to another, whether it’s within the same building or between different sites.
  • Add: Adding new devices to customer environment, configuring workstations and other devices.
  • Change: Changing the configuration or settings of existing IT assets, such as upgrading hardware, updating software, or modifying system configurations.
  • Dispose: Disposing of IT assets that are no longer needed or have reached the end of their useful life, including retiring old hardware and securely wiping data from storage devices.
