Service summary

Infrastructure management

The Service provides the tasks and activities needed to manage the Cegeka Cloud Housing (The Infrastructure) from a technical perspective. Cegeka unburdens the customer with all technical aspects for this infrastructure.

The Standard Service features are focussed to keep the Service up-and-running, to keep it secure and to optimize its technical parameters.

The Infrastructure

The Cegeka Cloud Housing Service offers physical locations for the installation of hardware in the Cegeka datacentres.

The physical location can be in the form of shared rack space, a closed dedicated rack or a cage. A cage is a closed fenced location in a Cegeka Datacentre which is only accessible by Cegeka Datacentre personnel and identified Customer contacts under supervision of Cegeka Datacentre personnel.

The physical location can be used to house Customer dedicated equipment (servers, network equipment, storage, etc.) and can provide redundant power, redundant network and redundant SAN connectivity.

Customer dedicated equipment:

  • all equipment residing in the Cegeka Datacentres is documented in the CMDB (CI), in which ownership (contact information) of the system is documented.
  • redundant power connection is possible by connection to 2 different power sockets (equipment needs to have 2 power supplies)
  • redundant network connectivity is possible by connection to 2 different access switches and fully redundant network connections on the equipment side (configured correctly) – there are no SPOFS with redundant network connections on a device
  • redundant SAN connectivity is possible by connection to 2 different SAN switches and fully redundant SAN boards on the equipment side (configured correctly) – there are no SPOFS with redundant SAN connections on a device
  • Power cabling is normally provided with the equipment
  • Network and SAN cabling is mandatory provided by Cegeka and will be charged to the Customer.

The Infrastructure includes the following components:

  • The physical location for the housing
  • 24/7 access control and security of the building and housing location
  • Redundant electrical installation (2 outlets) of the housing (electric distribution, generator, UPS system)
  • Inline UPS system that also protects the IT equipment from power surges and power peaks
  • Redundant climate control (heating, ventilation and air conditioning) of the housing – server inlet 22° ±2°, Relative humidity: 45% ± 15%
  • Fire detection and extinguishers of the housing – based on inert gas, not harmful for humans/equipment
  • Optional office space for the purpose of fallback in case of disaster recovery
  • Optional use of bladecenter infrastructure for the housing of Customer provided server blades
  • Certified Cegeka Datacentre personnel for all aspects above.

Cegeka Datacentres are geographically spread over 2 locations: one in Belgium (Hasselt) and one in the Netherlands (Geleen).

Cegeka Datacentres are Tier 3 certified, guaranteeing high availability, redundancy, operational continuity and safety and security.

Cegeka provides also additional connectivity POP (Point of Presence) locations in Belgium (Zaventem) and the Netherlands (Oude Meer), where Customer network access endpoint equipment can be placed.

The Cegeka Datacentres and POPs are interconnected by Cegeka private and redundant data connection lines. Bandwidth is continuous monitored to assure overcapacity for traffic.

Please note that the locations and interconnection of the Cegeka Datacentres and POPs may change over time. Such changes are always communicated upfront to relevant Customers and do not impact the nature of this Service.

Service features

Standard Service features

Infrastructure knowledge

Cegeka ensures skilled and certified engineers with experience on all housing components in order to perform the Service. Cegeka works together with contracted vendors to provide continuity on these components.

Technical setup

Cegeka continue evaluates and adapts the housing environment to ensure optimal technical setup (racks, cabling, physical security measurements, power supply, etc.).

Cegeka advises the Customer on housing needs and proposes optimal solutions for each case.

Cegeka performs and maintains the technical installation of the Infrastructure ensuring its correct working in accordance with Tier 3 Datacentre standards.

Technical setup is documented on operational level but is not available to the Customer. It can be subject to formal Customer auditing upon prior announcement. Documents can be shown but are not distributed.

Technical management

Cegeka is responsible for the technical operation of the Infrastructure and to assure it’s correct running during the agreed availability windows.

Housekeeping

Recurrent maintenance activities (housekeeping) are needed to ensure the correct working of the Infrastructure. Automation is applied where possible.

Proactive management

Cegeka performs housekeeping activities on all aspects of the housing service.

Resolution actions are triggered when needed and followed to assure the SLA.

Monitoring

Cegeka monitors the correct working of the housing infrastructure on a 24/7 basis (power, climate, access, etc.) Alerts are treated and appropriate resolution actions are triggered and followed to assure the SLA.

Security and Compliance

Physical security

The housing locations and the Cegeka Datacentre and POP locations are permanent physically secured and monitored.

Access is strictly arranged by the Cegeka Access Control procedure:

    • Technicians have to require access at least 24 hours upfront
    • Customer visits have to be requested at least 5 days upfront
    • Cegeka evaluates the access/visit requests
    • Visits are always logged with proof of identity
    • Visitors and Technicians are always accompanied by classified Cegeka DC personnel

Management of technical users

The management of technical users (for engineers) is performed following the Cegeka internal security policies and the contractual Customer security policies.

Detection of security incidents

The security management is performed following the Cegeka internal security policies and the contractual Customer security policies.

Security incidents are created in case a security breach is detected and is immediately communicated to the Customer and Cegeka stakeholders.

SIEM

SIEM is part of the Cegeka internal service and not deliverable to the Customer.

Underlaying Platform hardening

Not applicable for this Service.

Customers responsibility

The Customer remains responsible for the equipment placed in the housing locations. This may be by the Customer itself, a Third Party or by an adjacent Cegeka Service.

Requests

Both the Customer and Cegeka can issue a Request for Change (RFC) or a Request for Information (RFI).

There are multiple types of requests:

  • Service Request
    • This is a pre-defined request
    • Some can be requested via Horizon: these are pre-approved and pre-defined requests
    • Service request that is not pre-approved will be executed after approval by the CAB
    • Service Requests are executed as a change ticket and will be delivered within the indicated lead time.
    • The pre-defined execution is optimal automated.
    • This is used for requests that are often needed (multiple times per month).
    • These have a fixed price.
  • Normal Changes :
    • these are other non pre-defined requests
    • they need an evaluation and analysis where Cegeka needs to deliver a proposal and impact analysis to address the change request (with a T&M estimation).
    • A successive review, approval and planning (maintenance window) by a CAB of the proposal is needed prior to execution.
    • These are billed on T&M basis.

The handling of Requests is subject to the regular change management process (see Cross-functionals).

RACI role definition:

  • R-Responsible: The party who is responsible for the execution of the activity.
  • A- Accountable: The party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by the Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. The Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by the Responsible. The Accountable is by default reactive.
  • C-Consulted: The party who must be consulted and must approve or provide input prior to execution. This party (partly) gives direction to the result. This is about two-way communication.
  • I-Informed: That is the person/party who is informed about the decisions, progress and results achieved, so that the next step can be taken. This is about one-way communication.
