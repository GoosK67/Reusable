Standard Service Description

Introduction

End-User Experience Management provides insight into the performance and end user experience of the digital workplace. Together with visibility of devices, operating systems, virtual environments, and networks we create a unified, real-time view of end user experience.

This service not only monitors the resources an employee interacts with, but also takes it a step further: it enables the ability to proactively remediate issues to improve employees’ digital experience.

End User Experience Management focuses purely on employee experience while adding AI-driven analytics, scoring, sentiment gathering, and automated remediation. Just as the field of Customer Experience has grown to address the full range of customer interactions, End User Experience Management now serves the distinct needs of the IT organization in monitoring and improving employees’ digital experience. It combines technical metrics and employee sentiment to provide a complete view and objective measurement of employee experience across all resources.

At Cegeka, we provide End-User Experience Monitoring & Management (EUEM) as a Managed Service to incorporate Digital Experience into the customer strategy and processes.

By identifying, monitoring & tracking the end-user digital experience, and implement a continuous service improvement plan and continuously ‘put the finger to the wrist’, we are able to create a sustainable & productive end-user work environment.

Our approach

The steps are as follows:

  • Step 1: Establish a baseline by measuring and quantifying the digital experience. Determine issues affecting the employee experience by looking at the data. This data will enable you to establish a baseline and benchmark going forward.
  • Step 2: Engage with employees and collect smart feedback. Figure out what your employee’s digital experience is from their point of view.
  • Step 3: Stabilize your existing environment. Determine what steps you need to take to remediate the issues and track how you are improving over time.
  • Step 4: Develop your roadmap for change and optionally create a dedicated User Experience Office.
  • Step 5: Move from an SLA to an XLA. In the future, Cegeka and customer could revise our outmoded service level agreements (SLAs) to provide innovative growth.

Establishing the baseline – the metrics

A great Digital Experience should connect all the significant parts of an organization to fully understand the employee experience. Below, a high-level view is presented on the different components that go into calculating the digital experience score of the customer.

The most important and actual items that make up the score and the data types used to calculate are highlighted: 

  • Endpoint score: Hard metrics. This data tells you how well the device is performing. The data is correlated over buckets of time so it is possible to relate where they were working from and how their experience was different depending on their location or time.
  • Application score: Hard metrics and sentiment data are collected for business applications. Through baselining you can gain insight into usage of the business applications and where to improve to increase the productivity.
  • Collaboration score: Hard metrics are collected for the collaboration score. The collaboration score looks at the call quality of Teams and Zoom as in a hybrid working environment this is a critical factor for being productive.
  • User Sentiment: to measure how well IT is doing the user sentiment is linked in the DEX score. Without continuously measuring the actual user sentiment it is impossible to improve their satisfaction of IT or know the issues they have.

 

 

What results are we, Cegeka and the Customer, able to deliver after we implemented the Digital Experience Score? By connecting above system metrics with employee sentiment, you will have the following: 

    • Increased visibility of your landscape to reduce your incidents, whether an employee has raised a help desk ticket or not. 
    • An end-to-end view of the user experience. 
    • Prioritized recommendations to deliver actionable insights, based on both objective and subjective insights. 
    • Powerful automation capabilities to allow you to focus on higher-value activities. 
    • Real-time analytics that let the Customer make high-level decisions. 
    • Detailed diagnostics for lightning-fast troubleshooting. 
    • Employee feedback to provide a reality-based view of what the employee is experiencing day-to-day

Engaging with Employees – Changing the game

Companies now agree that being able to get direct and continuous feedback from employees is crucial. And being able to smartly corroborate that feedback with device-level details is game-changing. Having access to the best of both worlds enables IT to stop incidents before they become significant problems.

Getting feedback produces several benefits for the employees and the company as a whole. It allows IT to:

  1. Prioritize the correct issues that are important to employees and not IT.
  2. Get higher response rates from campaigns rather than relying on email surveys that very few employees respond to.
  3. Proactively solve problems before they create more significant problems.
  4. Only send the employee the campaigns that are related to them and when the time is convenient for them to respond.

By communicating directly with your employees, you’ll get a holistic view of their technical environment and their feelings about it. The key to collecting quality feedback is to set up, together, an engagement campaign that elicits actionable information.

Collecting employee sentiment, bringing it in correlation with the ‘tangible’ metrics will not only deliver the Digital User Experience Score, but will also:

  1. Identifying frustrated employees
  2. Uncover Shadow IT.
  3. Increase employee satisfaction and improve the productivity of employees.
  4. Bring non-IT issues to the front
  5. Make IT transparent and approached in a positive way

Stabilize existing Environment: Self-Healing remediation

To stabilize the environment in function of the Digital Experience Score and to tackle the employees’ technical problems and to react faster is to embed a Digital Employee Experience platform that provides us with the capabilities discussed earlier in this chapter:

  • Providing visibility & analytics.
  • Establish a targeted engagement channel.

But the remediated actions should also be part of stabilizing the existing environment and improving the end-user experience.

Deploying automated self-healing remediation: Automated self-healing allows us to solve problems in just a few clicks. The desired state of computing is maintained by continuous monitoring. If a problem is detected, it is resolved by automating a solution in real-time without disturbing the user. Providing IT with customizable scripts can trigger one-click fixes to prevent or resolve issues quickly. For example, if an app crashes, a self-healing app would automatically detect the program’s crash, delete the log file, and restart it. Automating the process reduces the number of reported incidents and increases end-user productivity.

One great benefit of automated remediation is that this will result in reduced incidents perceived by employees and faster resolution times, which is, directly part of our service desk Shift-2-Left & Shift-Out vision. For the tickets that do get submitted, you’ll have a quicker resolution without escalating the issue. Without spending time on unnecessary call center tickets, IT is freed up to focus on strategy and not put out fires.

Organizing a User Experience Office (Optional)

To accommodate this data (the combination of employee sentiment and hard metrics), Cegeka will setup a ‘virtual’ user experience office to report and solve performance problems as they impact the employee experience. The user experience office will communicate up and down the customer hierarchy (of course with respect to the established governance) in order to identify trends and fix incidents before they become big problems. One of the most important tactical fora to present the results of the data correlation will be the change advisory boards.

The experience office has the standard process of collecting the user experience score, reporting to the governance layers, resulting in user adoption programs and/or change requests (projects) in order to increase the user experience. After execution of the program/project, the monitoring will reveal the impact of the change in the organization, with a positive increase of the user experience score.

As you’ve probably noticed, the user experience office and the data collection + correlation does NOT only make use of the user experience management platform (tooling) but will also correlate this data (hard metrics & sentiment) with other end-user impacted services like data analytics from the service desk and feedback from program and project management.

Move from SLA to XLA (optional)

The conventional service level agreement (SLA) is a commitment to Customer that sets standards that Cegeka must maintain concerning production, uptime, speed, volume, and so on. SLAs have likely had no, or barely any, involvement from end-users. This is one of the biggest mistakes you can make when devising XLAs. Experience level agreements should be directly linked to end-user experience; therefore, it is pivotal that you involve them in XLA discussions. 

Another common mistake is using a “one-size fits all” approach, similar to what has been found in SLA usage. XLAs need to be tailored to experience and organizations problems or needs. Furthermore, SLAs focus on what you can physically measure: time, budgets, tickets etc. This completely misses the most important thing - the end-users experience.

Although there is so much more to say about SLA & XLA, the differences and the benefits, the dos and don’ts, Cegeka is preparing the journey to onboard customers in the XLA driven outsourcing model. Though nothing is perfect we strive to be a partner into giving all employees their best possible experience and we would like to embrace this in a partnership – between the Customer & Cegeka – and actively involve the Customer in this journey and our tactical initiative to embed XLA based reporting into our service model.

What is the added business value?

End-user experience monitoring plays an instrumental role in a customer’s organization growth and success. By providing constantly monitoring for end-users, the IT teams have more visibility of the entire digital workplace. Using these key insights, Cegeka can now take proactive and predictive remediations using the powerful automation engine of this new proposed End-User Experience Monitoring platform. This new service will also allow Cegeka to make improvements as-needed as new insights will become visible through new dashboards and reporting. Putting in place new remediation processes, the overall user satisfaction will become higher.

With the platform The Customer will gain a holistic understanding of the entire IT landscape that support your business. Having only infrastructure monitoring is not sufficient anymore to understand the relation of your applications and its underlying services. Incidents that manifest themselves as performance degradation on a user’s device can often be traced back to underlying infrastructure issues.

Improve Employee Productivity

By monitoring endpoint devices, the End-User Experience Monitoring (EUEM) service problems can be detected faster and if feasible, will be solved automatically. It will help to mitigate the impact of configurations changes, changes to the end-user environment. By implementing use cases specified on impact, effort and time lost we can accurately track the gained productivity.

Improve User Satisfaction through Proactive Problem Management

Employee engagement is improved by continuously capturing employee sentiment, scoring performance and automated remediation. The results are then investigated and taken into account when discussing issues and new remediations.

Faster problem resolution

By monitoring end-user experience proactively, the service will track the most critical transactions and will prevent impact on the end-users. The data that comes along with this monitoring service will help with an improved root cause analysis and will bring real measurements to the service desk. This will in turn improve the MTTI/MTR of the service desk and will help solve other issues the employee is experiencing at the same time.

Augmentation of operational insights and visibility

By augmenting the data of the EUEM, greater insights will improve the experience and help improve users’ satisfaction. The additional dashboards and reports will provide additional insights in the digital experience of the end-user and the IT landscape of the customer.

Better correlation of issues

EUEM is focused on understanding user experiences through interaction with applications. The analytics and data that will become available can also be used to monitor the performance of business applications or can detect new insights in recurring issues, e.g., for example the network area. These insights will help with inventory lists or hardware refreshes because of performance issues.

Cross-domain and business level monitoring strategy

The EUEM will complement all the areas of IT monitoring. Performance visibility gaps will be detected from different perspectives and monitoring will not cover only the underlying infrastructure but also the internals of the applications.

Measuring is an important first step, but it is even more important to do something with the insights you get through this Digital Experience Score. What is going well or badly? What works technically well, but still gets a low score in terms of user experience? This is of course the real value of this monitoring.

Standard Governance recurring activities:

  • Discuss DEX results and new findings (monthly)
  • Prioritise and set in motion initiatives to raise the DEX (monthly)
  • Report on value created (quarterly)

Calculation of necessary endpoint licenses

This service includes the subscriptions of the End-User Experience monitoring platform and the service activities described in chapter 1.4. The pricing is per endpoint in scope of Cegeka Management +2% (with a minimum of 25) for Lifecycle Management rounded up to a multiple of 100 with a minimum of 500.

For example:

A customer has 1066 endpoints in his environment. 1066 +2% (21.32) = 1087.32.

As a minimum 25 licenses are needed for LCM so the total comes to 1091. The number of licenses to need would be 1100.

The End-User Experience Management is part of the governance, see chapter 1.4.2 where pricing for the governance roles will be included in the governance pricing (and thus excluded from this SKU pricing)
