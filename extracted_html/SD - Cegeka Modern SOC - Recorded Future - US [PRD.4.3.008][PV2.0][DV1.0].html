Service description

Definitions

The following definitions shall have the meanings hereby respectively assigned to them.

“Business Hours”: Monday through Friday from 8:00 to 18:00 EST

“Horizon” : Customer Portal provided by Cegeka for reporting and change management

“Initial Priority” : The priority which is pre-assigned automaticity to a Security Alert specific to a technology.

“SOC/CSIRT Analyst” : Member of the SOC performing Incident Response activities

“Security Alert” : A notification of a potential security anomaly as detected by one or more of the SOC detection technologies which requires further investigation.

“Security Incident” : A violation of security policies based upon a human reported event or a generated Security Alert.

“Service” : Managed Detection & Response services

Operational Readiness

The goal of Operational Readiness is that both customer and Cegeka have a clear understanding of the governance and operational processes related to the Managed Detection & Response service.

Cegeka will perform the following activities during the Operational Readiness:

  • Agreement on the project governance containing:
    • Project Plan
    • Ready For Service (RFS)
  • Align on Incident Response Plan procedures.
  • Exchange information required to perform the Service.
  • Agreement on asset scope information
  • Agreement on (automated) response actions

The Operational Readiness will be formalized on a per project basis in a Customer Scoping Document. This document will include all the operational agreements related to the Service.

The Customer is responsible for informing Cegeka of any changes which may impact the Service.

RFS - Ready-for-Service

The RFS indicates that all the prerequisites are completed during the transition period to start the Service. This is the moment Cegeka will perform the Managed Services on the full contracted scope and invoicing will start accordingly.

The prerequisites for the RFS are:

Prerequisites

  1. Customer Scoping document is ready

To reduce the operational pressure for the customer, a one-month learning period is applied after the start of the RFS date to fine-tune the environment to reduce the number of false positives. This implies that all Security Alerts will only be handled during Business Hours until the learning period has ended, and no SLAs are measured. After this period, the Service will go into full operational mode including all SLA.

The RFS date has a maximum term of three (3) months after signing the contract unless explicitly agreed otherwise.

Managed Services

Cegeka works with a standardized “Security Incident Response Plan (S-IRP)” which describes how Cegeka handles information Security Incidents as part of the Managed Detection & Response Service to Customers.

Every occurrence of a Security Alert potentially resulting in a Security Incident is handled logically as described in this plan. The assignment of roles & responsibilities as well as the services and products available to Cegeka within a specific context will determine which parts of the plan will be executed by Cegeka, its customer or 3rd parties mandated by either or both.

This document describes the entire process, but the contractual agreements and Security Customer Scoping Document (S-CSD) will determine which of the process phases and activities will be handled by Cegeka under which conditions.

Advanced Threat Intelligence

Our SOC platform incorporated Advanced Threat Intelligence feeds by default. This helps improve real-time detection of the latest emerging threats and a more effective investigation of Security Alerts/Incidents.

Based upon the nature and type of threat, the following activities will be done to improve detection:

  • Identity threats by matching known Indicators of Compromise (IOC’s) like IP, hashes, URLs, e-mails, and certificates based upon the available data sources.
  • Receive periodic insights into active threats and threat actors to be aware on a global scale.
  • Periodically provide Threat News updates on new Tactics, Techniques and Procedures (TTP) which are used by Threat Actors

Based upon the nature and type of Security Alerts/Incidents, the following enrichments will be done:

  • Add Threat Context to involved artifacts like IP, hashes, URLs, e-mails, and certificates.
  • Verify IOC’s (hashes, IP and URLs) against multiple IOC specific threat intel’s to check for false positives.
  • Add Risk scores (0-100) to IOC to better estimate the potential risk.

24x7 Security Monitoring

The goal of Security Monitoring is to continuously monitor the customer’s environment based upon the scoped technologies and uncover potential security incidents which need to be act upon.

Cegeka will perform the following activities regarding Security Monitoring:

  • Monitor the customer’s environment based upon the scoped technologies to detect new Security Alerts
  • Enrich Security Alerts according to the available data sources.
  • Assign a SOC analyst to investigate Security Alert according to the Initial Priority.
  • Perform Triage on Security Alerts to identify Security Incidents. This includes the following steps:
    • Investigate the Security Alert to filter out false positives.
    • Assign a Security Incident Priority based upon the Security Incident Priority definitions.
    • Classify the Security Incident Area
  • Perform in-depth investigation using the scoped technologies to determine the best containment action or advice to take.
  • Document the Security incident in a Security Incident ticket.
  • Notify the customer according to the communication plan.

For Cegeka to able to effectively perform Security Monitoring, the customer is responsible for:

  • Notify Cegeka if he believes a Security Incident is occurring relevant to the scope of the Service.
  • Provide cooperation and contractability when required to assess the nature of the Security Incident.
  • Notify Cegeka if any changes in the environment are occurring which may have an impact on the performance of the Service.

Security Incident Area

Each Security Incident will be classified with a Security Incident Area which represents the type of Security Incident which best suits the events seen during our investigations.

Cegeka uses 17 standardized Security Incident Areas to classify Security Incidents:

Databreach

Definition

Accidental or purposeful disclosure of information related involving personal data

Examples

  • Confidential documents containing contact details are left unattended
  • Confidential information containing customer user accounts is accessed by unauthorized individuals
  • Confidential information containing employee data has leaked outside of the organization

Disclosure of information

Definition

Accidental or purposeful disclosure of information NOT containing any personal data

Examples

  • Confidential documents are left unattended.
  • Confidential information is accessed by unauthorized individuals
  • Confidential information has leaked outside of the organization

Loss or theft of equipment

Definition

Loss or theft of any device/medium that contains Customer-related data

Examples

  • Loss of laptop
  • Theft of storage device
  • Theft of hardcopy documents

Reconnaissance

Definition

Reconnaissance activities which have been observed on a device or the network. Reconnaissance activities are typically low-interaction and relying upon normal operation of a device/network and the information it discloses. Typically, no malicious payload is sent.

Examples

  • Scanning of a website
  • Port scanning of a server without any attempt to access the underlying services

Malware

Definition

Infection of a device with malicious software

Examples

  • Ransomware message appears on screen
  • Unusual behaviour after file download or website visit (slowness, unexpected moving mouse pointer, unexpected windows appearing, ...)

Technical intrusion

Definition

(Possibility of) access to data or devices without authorization, possibly followed by malicious activities.

This incident area is also used whenever a combination of more than one incident type is identified.

Examples

  • Effective access to unauthorized device/application/database
  • Unauthorized physical access to IT devices/locations

Policy violation

Definition

An end-user has performed an action that is against the Customer’s security policy

Examples

  • Installation of unallowed/unwanted software (games, cryptominer, ...)
  • Exhibition of illicit behaviour (inappropriate website visits, illegal downloads, ...)

Denial-Of-Service

Definition

Purposeful disruption or degradation of IT services

Examples

  • Internet-facing website partially or fully unavailable
  • VPN connection slow or impossible

Vulnerability

Definition

Detection of presence/exploitation of a known vulnerability

Examples

  • Report of vulnerability present in the information system
  • Vulnerability exploitation detected by device or end-user

Social engineering

Definition

Detection of (attempted) attacks on information systems by use of non-technical or low-technology means - such as lies, impersonation, tricks, bribes, blackmail, and threats.

Used for each such detection except phishing, for which a separate incident area exists

Examples

  • Phone calls with falsified impersonation to trick a user to click a link
  • Phone call with falsified impersonation pressuring an employee to disclose/send information to the caller
  • Chat request asking to execute an action for which the requester isn’t authorized

Probing

Definition

Detection of attempts of sending actual payloads to a system with the objective to learn something about the system or exploit the system. Probing often follows initial reconnaissance.

Examples

  • Requests to port 1433 to detect the presence of SQL server and gather details about the SQL Server version, configuration and database service components
  • Web application scans trying to detect specific vulnerabilities on an application

Brute forcing

Definition

Detection of activities using a cryptanalysis technique or other kind of attack method involving an exhaustive procedure that tries all possibilities, one-by-one.

Examples

  • Multiple and high frequency authentication attempts to access a server with a built-in account

Compliance

Definition

Detection of a technical non-compliance with security baselines

Examples

  • The use of a built-in administrator account which should be disabled/locked
  • Interruption of log forwarding towards SIEM

Phishing

Definition

Report of e-mail with malicious content

Examples

  • E-mail with malicious file attachment
  • E-mail with malicious hyperlink
  • E-mail with fraudulent content

Identity theft

Definition

Detection of events indicating someone wrongfully obtains and uses another person’s personal data in some way that involves fraud or deception

Examples

  • Detection of successful simultaneous logons with a personal account from different locations

SOC

Definition

A yet to be categorized security incident to be handled by Cegeka SOC. This incident area is used for monitoring alerts

on security related services. Tickets should never be closed with this area selected.

Examples

N/A

Other

Definition

A security incident that does not fit into any of the other Security Incident Area

Examples

N/A – all known Security Incident Area should fit into one of the other areas at the time of writing

The Security Incident Area can change during the Security Incident Response Process.

Threat Response

The goal of Threat Response is to contain an active Security Incident to reduce further impact on the customer’s environment. A threat Response only includes containment actions and does not include any activities related to eradicating malicious files or recovering impacted systems.

Containment actions must always be approved by the customer before Cegeka can execute the recommended actions. The approval can either be “pre-approved” or ad-hoc.

  • In case the containment action is pre-approved, Cegeka will execute the recommended containment action(s) and will notify the customer.
  • In case the containment action is not pre-approved, Cegeka will request ad-hoc approval and will wait for the customer’s approval before execution the recommended action(s). If executed, the customer will be notified.

Based upon the nature of the Security Incident, the following response action(s) can be executed in the customer’s environment. Each response action has a technical requirement to be available for the customer.

Domain takedown

A domain takedown is a request issued to an ISP, web host, social media company, domain registrar, or mobile app store operator to remove malicious or fraudulent content or domains abusing the Customer’s brand.

In such a case, the Customer can request such a takedown, but it required to provide explicit permission in the form of a Letter of Appointment.

A credit system is used for takedowns. These takedown credits need to be purchased upfront. Each takedown is one credit. Credits are debited only upon successful takedown. Unused credits expire at the end of the contract period and do not roll over.

In order to use Domain Takedown, the following is required:

requirements

  1. MDR for Brand Intel Service
  1. Take down credits
  1. Signed Letter of Appointment

Cyber Security Advisor

The Cyber Security Advisor is the point of contact for all tactical and strategic security topics and acts as a liaison between Customer’s security stakeholders and Cegeka.

The Cyber Security Advisor has the following responsibilities:

  • Organize recurring security Service meetings.
  • Present the security report.
  • Advice on resilience recommendations to improve the security posture of the customer.
  • Available during Business Hours to contact via email or phone.
  • Analyze Security Incidents on a monthly base and provide feedback or actions when required.

Governance

Process

Authorized Contacts

For security reasons, only people identified as Authorized Contact are allowed to collaborate with our Cegeka SOC. Only they can interact with the Cegeka SOC. All other persons will automatically be prevented and will be notified towards the Customer. These are also the people who will receive the monthly report.

The Authorized Contacts will be formalized in the Customer Scoping document.

Operational & Escalation Contact

Any new Security Incident will result in a notification towards the customer Operational Contact(s).

If any blocking issues prevents Cegeka from continuing the Service as agreed, an escalation towards the customer Escalation Contact will be done.

The Operational & Escalation contact(s) will be formalized in the Customer Scoping document.

Decommissioning process

At the end of the MDR Service, Cegeka retains the right to:

  • Remove any of the Intellectual Property used during the Service to improve the overall quality.

At the end of the MDR Service, the customer retains the right to:

  • Gain full access to the management of customer owned technologies used within the MDR service.

At the end of the MDR Service, the customer has thirty (30) days from the date of termination to retain the right to:

  • Request a copy of the customer’s data to a designated location. Cegeka will transfer the data to the designated location owned by the customer. The data will contain all possible information which is technically possible to provide. This will be priced via Time and Material. Such requests must be submitted via e-mail. If Customer does not request the data within the 30-day period described above, Cegeka will permanently destroying all data no longer under a valid Service contract.
