Introduction

The rapid rise of remote work and the emergence of hybrid workplaces have highlighted the need for organizations to adapt their working styles. One of the key priorities is to ensure a seamless and enhanced collaboration experience that fosters greater engagement and efficiency in the Digital Workplace. As a result, there has been an accelerated adoption of cloud platforms like Microsoft Teams and SharePoint.

However, it has become evident that organizations often focus primarily on technical training for end users, neglecting the importance of addressing new ways of working and associated best practices. Consequently, users find themselves lost within the ecosystem, struggling to navigate the solutions in a desired and manageable manner. In some cases, users resort to ad hoc methods that may not align with governance, structure, and security requirements, potentially leading to mistakes with adverse implications.

From an end user perspective, it is crucial to offer a user-friendly process for requesting collaboration workspaces where team members can easily exchange documents, communicate, and access information. If this process becomes cumbersome, users may revert to familiar private context tools such as Dropbox, inadvertently engaging in shadow-IT practices that pose security and management risks for the organization. Therefore, it is essential to provide a solution that aligns with organizational policies while still delivering efficiency gains for end users.

Additionally, platform administrators often encounter challenges when it comes to managing the lifecycle of workspaces. Frequently, workspaces continue to exist even when they are no longer needed, such as completed projects, disbanded teams, or merged departments. It can be difficult for administrators to control this, as the responsibility lies with the workspace owner.

What is the added business value

Optimized, standardized, secured and automated workspaces

The Workspace Engine enables customers to integrate and digitize their processes within Microsoft 365 by utilizing SharePoint Online and Teams. By utilizing our Workspace Engine, customers gain the ability to generate multiple workspace templates based on their specific business and technical requirements. These templates are standardized and secure, ensuring consistency and protection. Moreover, our Workspace Engine automates the creation of these templates through various line of business applications, with or without user interaction.

Improved self-service, adoption and accelerated roll-out of workspaces

The shift left principle is effectively implemented in the Workspace Engine. Our Engine facilitates the rapid deployment of workspaces in SharePoint or Teams by empowering end users to create their own workspaces using predefined templates within the organization. This approach significantly expedites the utilization of the Microsoft 365 platform and enhances the adoption of the solution. By eliminating the need for end users to wait for the organization to roll out workspaces, the Workspace Engine enables immediate access and fosters a more efficient workflow.

Overcome silos by working together in virtual and physical teams

The Workspace Engine offers an excellent solution to address the challenge of information silos within organizations. Many organizations struggle with cross-departmental or cross-team collaboration due to uncertainties regarding data storage. The Workspace Engine is specifically designed to support various business processes within an organization, such as "Projects," "Sales," "Dossiers," and more. It ensures that end users from different departments or teams can collaborate effectively by providing them with a unified workspace dedicated to a common goal. This eliminates confusion about where to store data and fosters seamless collaboration across the organization, ultimately breaking down information silos.

Empowered employees and unburdened IT-department

The Workspace Engine operates as a self-service solution, enabling end users to create workspaces independently without requiring assistance from the IT department. Through seamless integration with SharePoint, Teams, and other compatible line of business tools, end users can conveniently request a workspace. Within a matter of minutes, they gain access to a standardized workspace, allowing them to start working promptly. This self-service capability frees up the IT department's resources, allowing them to concentrate on enhancing the workspace, refining governance practices, and other critical aspects of workspace management.

Updates

Our solution is provided through a SaaS (Software as a Service) model. Regular updates containing bug fixes and new functionalities will be deployed at scheduled intervals, and you will be promptly informed about these changes. It's important to note that there will be no additional costs associated with the deployment of general updates.

However, please keep in mind that there might be separate charges for impact analysis and any required development work related to tailored functionalities. Please refer to the "Extensive Functionalities" and "Financial Information" sections for more details on this matter.

Our solution is compatible with both on-premises installations of SharePoint Server and cloud services in Microsoft 365. For on-premises installations, automatic deployment of updates, fixes, and new functionalities to the client's environment is not available. However, upon request, Cegeka can install a new On-Premises version to ensure that the client has access to the latest version of the engine.

Architecture

In case you use a SharePoint On-Premises environment, the Workspace Engine will be installed on your local environment. A service bus will receive information from SharePoint at a regular time about new sites that must be created.

If you are using Office 365, your environment will connect with the Azure environment of Cegeka. After all, Cegeka offers this Engine as a SaaS Cloud solution.
