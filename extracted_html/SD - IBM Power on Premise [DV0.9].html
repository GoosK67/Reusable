Service description

Service summary

The IBM Power on premise service is an IBM power system management service for the administration of IBM power LPARs/hardware running that are running on premise.

Service description: features and functionalities

The IBM Power on premise service contains remote or on-site management of IBM Power and related hardware. As well it includes monitoring, patching, migrating, etc. of the hardware.

Standard services

Cegeka provides the standard services listed below, however the SLA will determine:

  • whether a customer is entitled to a particular service
  • the frequency of execution of a service

Standard we provide reactive support based on tickets or project support.

Governance

  • See service description Governance (Bert Prevaes)

Technical

The IBM Power on Premise service can be via on-site support or remote support. This is a re-active service.

On-site support

With on-site support, the system engineer will carry out his work at the customer’s site. Also known as site-visit. Afterwards a site-visit report will be created summarizing all actions performed.

Remote support

With remote support, the system engineer will carry out his work remotely. Afterwards a report will be created summarizing all actions performed. The remote connectivity can be provided by Cegeka or the customer (VPN/RDP/etc).

Privileged Identity Management

"Privileged Identity management" (PIM) in Cegeka or in other words “password management” for privileged users, is provided by a Thycotic Secret server solution. This server hosts all authentication information that is required to make a connection to <Company> IT platform. Secret Server enables Cegeka to automate the management and security of your enterprise privileged accounts from a single console, saving time and money while reducing risk.

Remote platform management and steppingstone network

For security and compliancy reason Cegeka uses a User Access Gateway (UAG) and a “Management (Tooling) servers” that are separated onto different VMs. In this setup, the customer environment will contain one Windows or Linux based UAG (Remote Desktop Gateway) and one or more management (tooling) servers. Using a RD gateway, we can pass-through the server to connect directly into the customer environment. With this setup, aka steppingstone environment, Cegeka employees can reach <Company>’s production environment. A stepping-stone is a security measure that channels different logical security mechanisms sequentially (instead of one next to another). This makes the steppingstone the only access point for system- and application engineers for <Company>’s environment. This guarantees the necessary access controls for <Company>’s environment to be ISAE3402 compliant by having:

  • Granular control on every ‘stone’ or security mechanism in the chain.
  • A central point to log all activities. This can be used in incident management, for security audits or when performing a root-cause analysis.
  • A unique identification of the system- and application managers.
  • Exclusion of shared passwords (with high privileges).

This controlled and uniform access will result in a more stable environment.

Cegeka uses a separate network segment from which secure access to <Company>’s computing platform and Infrastructure components can be established using a Terminal Server. This connection method ensures a controlled and secure access to <Company>‘s production environment.

System guard

The System guard service ensures access to Cegeka’s 3rd support level on a 7x24x365 basis for emergency (P1) incidents. Prerequisite for the System guard service is that the Cegeka support specialist (the ‘System guard’) can access the affected system (or systems) via an S2S-VPN or IP-VPN connection.  The System guard will come on-site when the incident cannot be handled via the remote connection.

The remote System guard will start handling the incident within 1 hour after the System guard engineer has been called by the Cegeka Technical Support Centre. In case on-site presence is required, Cegeka guarantees that the support specialist will be on-site within 8 hours after the decision for on-site intervention has been taken (excluding travel time and for sites within the Benelux).

System guard services are performed outside business hours which are described in <Comments>.

The pricing of the System guard service is based on a fixed standby fee per month. All interventions, remote or on-site, will be charged separately. The applicable rates are those for emergency requests as specified in Schedule E – Pricing and invoicing of <Comments>.

Availability Management

The availability of the LPAR(s) and the applications/services required by the customer can be monitored by a monitoring tool.

Single LPAR is a non-redundant platform where the customer data and system configurations are protected by local backup procedures.

Dual LPAR service can provide support for high availability for the following flavours:

  • Hardware replication (at storage level)
  • Software replication
  • Power HA

Data Protection as a Service (DPaaS)

The Cegeka Managed Availability Solutions consist of several capabilities to increase the availability of <Company>’s IT infrastructure.

The solutions are described in this service schedule:

  1. Bare Metal Restore Service (BMR)[1]

This service consists of the temporary provisioning by Cegeka of cold standby computing infrastructure to restore the critical IT functions of <Company> in case of disaster. Optionally, Cegeka can also provide temporary workplaces.

  1. Managed High Availability Service (MHA)

This service consists of the installation, configuration, and management of mirroring software between a <Company>’s primary and backup system, including switch-over to the backup system in case of critical failures.

  1. Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS)

This service consists of the provisioning by Cegeka of a Disaster Recovery (DR) solution including a public Cloud based standby computing infrastructure, to restore the critical IT functions of <Company>, in case of disaster. This service also includes the target environment with storage repositories and all DR software licenses. For every business-critical server, an active backup server can be installed on the public cloud that can - in case of a disaster - take over the production services in no time.

  1. Backup as a service (BaaS)

This service consists of the installation, configuration, and management of a Data Domain at the <Company’s> site to create local backups. This backup data will be replicated to a Data Domain at Cegeka. With this service the data will be kept accessible or restorable from a remote location on case of an outage or failure.

User & Access Management

Cegeka engineers need to have credentials with full rights to allow setup, configure and administer the LPAR. Users can be created, modified, or removed by Cegeka.

Administrator account will be managed by Cegeka in case of full support.

Patching

Based on the customer preferred frequency patching can be provided by Cegeka. The patching is performed following the customers internal patch schedule. Customers are informed about the possible impact of the patching activities following the standard communication schedule.

Patching of HMC, SAN, Storage, VIOS, Tape and Data Domain.

Down time is required when set up is not redundant.

The customer will have to grant a maintenance window allowing to perform following activities that involve system unavailability:

  • Backup (system) data
  • Patching activities

In case of a dual LPAR setup with software replication, a combination of switch test and fix upgrade can be considered to minimize the downtime of the production system.

For devices at an outdated patch or software level Cegeka will provide this service on best effort.

Lifecycle Management (LCM)

Lifecycle management is included in the service as part of the Cegeka on premise service.

The LCM is performed following the Supplier LCM schedule. Customers are informed about the LCM schedule and possible impact of the LCM-activities following the standard communication schedule.

Life cycle management for HMC, VIOS, Tape, Data Domain, Storage, hardware, etc., can be performed. Life cycle management will follow the life cycle from the suppliers.

Life cycle management of non-supported firmware etc. will be performed on best effort basis.

Security

Critical hardware vulnerability patches will be applied asap in close cooperation with the customer based on standard changes.

Capacity Management

Capacity management is included in the service as part of the Cegeka Power on premise service.

Capacity management can be performed during periodical site visits or 24/7 based on monitoring support. The usage of the resources is measured by monitoring tools that keep track of historical data allowing to visualize trends. Additionally, predefined major alarm levels are defined to generate alerts when thresholds are exceeded. Based on these alerts and trends, the customer will be consulted to generate a list of appropriate actions (increase resources, clean-up, performance analysis, reengineering applications, etc).

Customers are informed about capacity activities only if customer impact is expected.

Performance management

Optional basic system performance can be monitored 24/7 using our monitoring tool.

Cegeka can help with system performance issues.

Hardware migration

Cegeka can assist with migrations to new hardware.

Monitoring & Reporting

Cegeka monitors the on-customer premises infrastructure and can perform corrective actions.

Cegeka will install and configure monitoring tools over relevant aspects of the environment. These tools will collect and send data to the system management tools of Cegeka that either <Company> or Cegeka can use for infrastructure management purposes such as incident, availability, capacity, and service level management or for license and billing reporting.

Monitoring includes the following aspects.

  • Collecting
    Cegeka’s monitoring platform will collect information on predefined KPI’s. These KPI’s are infrastructure related as well as service orientated.
  • Alerting
    Warning thresholds alerts upcoming capacity and performance issues.
    Failing services will be detected and notified on a unified dashboard.
  • Registration and resolving (Cegeka ticketing tool including CMDB)
    All reported incidents will be recorded in the Service management tool, analysed, and assigned to a resolver group. Depending on the agreement <Comments>, the resolver group will be facilitated by <Company>, Cegeka or a 3rd party.
  • Reporting
    Cegeka’s IT Service Management Platform (ITSM) monitors (and manages) the customers’ IT infrastructure and delivers real-time reports, which can be consulted online

Availability and utilization reports will be created against this pre-defined timetable on a monthly base and be available on the Customer Portal Site (Horizon).

The Monitoring services can be customized to generate alerts on user-customizable thresholds.

Monitoring overview

Solution Element

Description

Heartbeat

The Heartbeat monitoring will poll the in-scope systems and provide system availability data to the Cegeka Technical Support Center.

System Health Check

The System Health Check will monitor the resource utilization (e.g. memory, processor, disk, network load)

Application Service Check

The Application Service Check will perform monitoring, logging and reporting services for system and application software. Cegeka can develop scripts (on a time & material basis) to perform routine checks (e.g. processes, log files) on system and application software.

Hardware SNMP monitor

The SNMP monitor will monitor remote devices via SNMP (Simple Network Management Protocol), querying said devices for current status and alerts.

Notification Service: For every device that is recorded in Cegeka’s monitoring environment, Cegeka offers a 24X7/365 alert notification service. Each automatically reported incident - by the Cegeka monitoring environment - will be notified by email to <Company> or a by <Company> selected external partner.

Registration Service: For every device that is recorded in Cegeka’s monitoring environment or that has an active premium service, Cegeka offers a 24X7/365 alert registration service. Each automatically reported incident - by the Cegeka monitoring environment to the TSC - will be registered in the Cegeka ITSM tool and assigned to Cegeka, <Company>’s IT department or a by <Company> selected external partner. For every warning or error that can be alerted, the priority of the matching incident will be specified in agreement with <Company>.

S2S VPN

For monitoring a S2S-VPN, connection must be established from <Company> premises to the Cegeka datacentre. The S2S VPN tunnel can be ordered as an additional service with reference code IS-NWS-VFW-S2S. This service also includes a ping heartbeat check to ensure that the tunnel is up and running.

Reporting

The Cegeka standard reporting service consists of a monthly, automated reporting on incident, problem, and request management tickets. These reports are made available on a portal site.
