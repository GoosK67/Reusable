Standard Service Description

Introduction

Our Digital Wellbeing service is designed to enhance the employee experience within your organization by leveraging data-driven insights and actionable recommendations. By implementing this service, you can drive business success, increase employee engagement, enhance productivity, and improve overall digital wellbeing.

Through the use of Insights, we provide managers and leaders with visibility into team dynamics, enabling them to foster a sense of belonging, encourage effective communication, and create a positive work environment. This increased engagement leads to improved employee satisfaction and higher productivity levels.

With our service we analyse work patterns and offer personalized recommendations for time management, focused work, and learning opportunities. By optimizing productivity, employees can work more efficiently and effectively, leading to enhanced output and business performance.

In addition to the overall benefits of our Digital Wellbeing service, we focus on three key pillars to further enhance the employee experience within your organization:

  1. Individual Digital wellbeing: We recognize the importance of individual wellbeing and happiness in the workplace. By leveraging data-driven insights, we provide personalized recommendations. This empowers employees to better manage their workloads, prioritize tasks, and maintain a healthy work-life balance. By fostering individual happiness, employees are more likely to experience job satisfaction, engagement, and overall wellbeing.
  2. (Hybrid) Teamwork & Remote Habits: With the rise of hybrid work environments and remote collaboration, it's essential to foster effective teamwork and remote work habits. Our service provides managers and leaders with valuable visibility into team dynamics. This enables them to cultivate a sense of belonging, encourage effective communication, and create a positive work environment, regardless of physical location. By focusing on teamwork and remote habits, we promote collaboration, strengthen team bonds, and drive collective success.
  3. Leadership in a Digital Age: Effective leadership plays a crucial role in promoting digital wellbeing and driving organizational success. Our service equips leaders with the necessary tools and insights to become digitally savvy and empathetic leaders. By leveraging data from these Insights, leaders can gain a comprehensive understanding of their teams' work patterns, wellbeing trends, and engagement levels. Armed with this knowledge, leaders can make informed decisions, provide guidance, and support their teams in achieving optimal digital wellbeing and performance.

By aligning our Digital Wellbeing service with these three focus areas - individual happiness, (hybrid) teamwork & remote habits, and (digital) leadership - we enable organizations to create a holistic and thriving work environment. This, in turn, drives employee engagement, boosts productivity, and ultimately contributes to the overall success of the business.

What is the added business value?

Our Digital Wellbeing service offers significant added business value by aligning with our three key focus areas, ultimately contributing to the holistic improvement of the employee experience within your organization.

Enhanced employee productivity

By nurturing digital wellbeing, employees can experience an increase in their productivity, leading to a more harmonious work-life balance and a decrease in burnout. Insights provide valuable data on employee work patterns, collaboration trends, and focus time, allowing your organization to identify areas where employees may be facing challenges or excessive workloads. By providing training and guidance based on these insights, Cegeka can assist employees in optimizing their work habits, prioritizing tasks, and achieving a healthy work-life integration, resulting in increased productivity and efficiency within the organization.

Increased Employee Wellbeing and Engagement

Prioritizing digital wellbeing demonstrates a commitment towards employees' mental health and overall wellbeing. When employees feel supported and cared for, they are more likely to be engaged, motivated, and satisfied with their work. Insights can provide personalized recommendations to help employees establish healthy work habits, take breaks, and maintain a healthy work-life balance. By providing guidance based on individual needs, your organization can foster a positive work environment, leading to higher levels of employee engagement and satisfaction. This, in turn, can result in reduced turnover rates and increased employee retention.

Improved organizational Culture and Reputation

A focus on digital wellbeing contributes to the development of a positive organizational culture. When companies prioritize their employees' wellbeing, it fosters a sense of trust, support, and mutual respect within teams. By leveraging insights to identify potential sources of stress or inefficiency, organizations can proactively address these issues and create a healthier work environment. This commitment to digital wellbeing can also enhance your company’s employer branding by attracting top talent who value work-life balance and wellbeing. A positive organizational culture and a strong employer brand can lead to increased employee loyalty, positive word-of-mouth, and a competitive advantage in the job market. Enhanced Leadership Performance and Decision-Making

Digital wellbeing is not only beneficial for employees, but also for leaders who face increased demands and responsibilities in a digital age. By utilizing Insights to optimize digital habits, leaders can help improve productivity, focus, and creativity which are essential skills for effective leadership. Moreover leaders can set an example for their teams and inspire them to create a positive feedback loop that enhances the wellbeing of the entire organization. Additionally, by gaining access to data-driven insights on the digital wellbeing of their teams, leaders can make more informed decisions that support their employees' needs and preferences, leading to improved team performance, collaboration, and satisfaction. Therefore, digital wellbeing can be a powerful tool for leaders to enhance their own capabilities and empower their teams, resulting in better outcomes for the organization as a whole.

What is included in the service?

We highly value the significance of digital wellbeing and understand its impact on individuals and organizations. However, we adopt a customer-centric approach that acknowledges the diverse needs and preferences of each customer. Therefore, we do not offer recurring services by default, as we believe that the services should be tailored to the unique requirements of each customer. Standard Services

Data-driven reports to maximize the Productivity and Digital Wellbeing in your organization

In today's dynamic and ever-changing business landscape, the Digital wellbeing and productivity of your workforce are fundamental to success. With reporting we can provide you with valuable data-driven insights that can help your organization navigate the complexities of the modern workplace related to wellbeing and productivity.

As businesses adapt to hybrid work models understanding key metrics become essential. This service offers a comprehensive suite of metrics and analytics, allowing you to gain further insights into various aspects of your organization's productivity and wellbeing.

From assessing business resilience to understanding the impact of different actions on employee productivity, our service offers a holistic view of your organization's performance. By examining meeting effectiveness, manager effectiveness, and more, you can identify areas for improvement and make informed decisions to foster a healthier work environment.

These data driven reports based on your organisations data will allow you to make informed decisions instead of using general statistics. Important to know is the fact that privacy is guaranteed and inherent part of the set up of the employee experience platform and all the reports created by the platform.

The scope of the reporting service contains following elements:

  • Individual Digital wellbeing
    • How much time employees collaborate outside of set working hours or weekends.
    • How many hours on average are employees actively connected. Overview per department or team.
    • Average new hire internal network size after their first 3 months
    • Overview on % of employees by department/team who had the opportunity to follow learning events. This is based on the appointments in the calendars (keywords: “train”, “learn”, “course”, “introduction”, “onboarding”, “academy”, “education” or “brown bag”)
    • Is multitasking driven by habit or necessity?
    • Do employees have sufficient time to focus (sum of one-hour or longer blocks where an employee didn’t attend a meeting). Overview per department/team
    • How are employees handling urgency. Impact of mail and meetings with “urgent” keywords.
    • Do employees embrace the flexibility of start/end times of a working day?
  • (Hybrid) teamwork & remote habits
    • Overview of the weekly number of hours a person spent in meetings, emails, chats and calls by department/team
    • Overview of conflicting/overlapping meetings
    • Overview on how employees are working (on-site, remote or hybrid) per department/team
    • Overview on how many percent of their time employees multitask during a meeting (mails, chats, …) per department/team
    • Overview of the average internal employee network size in the last four weeks. With how many people did an employee on average had a meaningful interaction (e.g. sending mail, meeting, teams call…) per department/team
    • How much time do employees spend using digital collaboration tools?
  • Leadership in a Digital Age
    • Overview in percentage on how many employees have at least 50 minutes/month 1:1 meetings with their manager per department/team
    • Average weekly hours new hires are connected to with their Manager in the last 4 weeks per department/team
    • Are managers balancing oversight with employee autonomy?
    • Understand to what extent managers suffer from collaboration overload (mails, meetings…) per department/team
    • Overview of the Manager-direct report relationship based on the average meeting hours with direct manager present and average manager 1:1 time. Based on these insights we can see if people are under-coached, sufficient coached or highly managed

The outcome of this report provides valuable insights into the productivity and wellbeing of your organization's workforce:

  • Data-Driven decision making: By collecting these metrics, we can make informed decisions about how to improve digital wellbeing and productivity. It provides a data-driven approach to understanding the dynamics within your organization.
  • Identifying pain points: The report helps pinpoint specific areas where your organization may be facing challenges. For example, it can identify if employees are working long hours, suffering from collaboration overload, or struggling to find time for focused work.
  • Improving Work-Life Balance: Metrics related to working hours outside of set working hours or weekends can highlight potential issues with work-life balance. This data can be used to implement policies or initiatives that promote healthier work habits.
  • Enhancing manager-employee relationships: The report offers insights into the interactions between managers and employees. It can help determine if employees are having regular 1:1 meetings with their managers and whether these meetings are effective.
  • Optimizing meeting efficiency: Metrics on meeting effectiveness, multitasking during meetings and conflicting/overlapping meetings can inform strategies for optimizing meetings, reducing time spent in unproductive meetings, and improving overall efficiency.
  • Supporting hybrid work models: As businesses increasingly adopt hybrid work models, understanding how employees are adapting to remote or hybrid work becomes crucial. This report can shed light on the effectiveness of remote collaboration and its impact on productivity for your organization.
  • Fostering Learning and Development: By tracking employees' participation in learning events, you can assess your organization's commitment to employee development and identify areas for improvement in this area.
  • Enhancing Leadership Skills: Insights into the manager-direct report relationship can help identify whether managers are striking the right balance between oversight and autonomy. It can also highlight opportunities for leadership development and coaching.
  • Business Resilience: Assessing how well your organization is handling urgency and multitasking can provide insights into its ability to adapt and respond to rapidly changing business environments.
  • Benchmarking: We compare your organization's digital wellbeing metrics to industry benchmarks, helping you understand how you compare against other organizations of the same size.

Overall, the outcome of this report is a comprehensive understanding of your organization's strengths and opportunities in terms of digital wellbeing and productivity.

Assessing employee wellbeing through Digital Pulses

In today's fast-paced and digitally-driven work environment, the wellbeing of your workforce is very important. Our Digital Wellbeing Pulse assessments are designed to provide your organization with valuable insights into the digital wellbeing based on the feedback of your employees. This service helps you understand how digital tools and work habits impact the overall health and happiness of your workforce.

The scope of this pulse contains following topics:

  • Data Collection and Analysis: we collect relevant data on digital behaviours, work habits, and well-being indicators from your employees through surveys. These surveys are often based on the reporting results captured via the metrics as described in the previous chapter.
  • Customized Metrics: we tailor the Digital Pulse to your organization's specific needs, ensuring that we focus on the metrics most relevant to your business goals and well-being objectives. Together with your HR department, we define the questions so we can capture valuable insights.
  • Digital Wellbeing Index: the Digital Wellbeing Index is a measure of how digital technologies are affecting the wellbeing of your workforce. It is calculated based on a combination of factors, including work hours, multitasking, and after-hours collaboration. This index provides an overall understanding of the impact of digital technologies on the wellbeing of your employees and can help your organization identify areas for improvement and implement strategies to promote healthier digital habits and work-life balance..
  • Survey structure/scope
    • Define the End-User Survey:
      • Determine the purpose of the survey and the specific insights you want to gather.
      • Keep the survey concise with a maximum of ten questions to encourage participation.
      • Use a combination of closed-ended and open-ended questions to gather quantitative and qualitative data.
    • Determine the Sample Size:
      • Consider the size of your organization and the level of representation needed.
      • Calculate the appropriate sample size using statistical methods to ensure accurate results.
      • Aim for a diverse sample that includes users from different departments, roles, and experience levels.
    • Structure the Survey:
      • Arrange the questions in a logical order that minimizes dropouts.
      • Start with simple and basic questions to capture initial responses and engage participants.
      • Place more complex questions in the middle when respondents are more invested in the survey.
      • End with simple and basic questions to maintain participation and conclude on a positive note.

The outcome of the Digital Wellbeing Pulse Assessment provides valuable insights into the digital wellbeing of your employees. The assessment collects data on digital behaviours, work habits, and wellbeing indicators, and calculates a Digital Wellbeing Index to provide an overall measure of how digital technologies are affecting your workforce's wellbeing. Based on the assessment results we can offer workshops and training sessions to promote healthier digital habits and work-life balance. These initiatives can help improve employee satisfaction, productivity, and overall health and happiness.

Transformation of insights into actions

It is the unique combination of factual data-driven reports from your organisation and the insights from the employees which will allow you to take actions based on facts instead of assumption. Our assessment includes actions for improving digital wellbeing and productivity within your organization. These actions are tailored to address specific opportunities identified in the data-driven insights and pulses. Our focus is on three key areas: individual digital happiness, (hybrid) teamwork and remote habits, and leadership in a digital age.

Below you can find the Transformation Actions based on the outcome of the data-driven insights and pulses:

  • Digital Wellbeing and Productivity action plan: a comprehensive action plan that outlines specific steps, initiatives, and strategies to improve digital wellbeing and productivity. This plan will be customized to align with the unique needs and goals of your organization. The Action Plan can contain following components:
    • Individual Digital Happiness: we tailor actions to boost individual digital happiness, ensuring employees find fulfilment and satisfaction in their digital experiences.
      • Digital Balance: strategies for achieving a harmonious balance between digital engagement and personal well-being are incorporated into the comprehensive action plan.
      • Deconnect the Digital Brain: guidelines to detach from constant digital connectivity and foster periods of disconnection for mental rejuvenation are integrated into the action plan.
    • (Hybrid) Teamwork & Remote Habits: focused actions to optimize teamwork and cultivate positive remote work habits are outlined, recognizing the unique dynamics of hybrid teams.
      • Smart Meetings: incorporating intelligent meeting strategies to enhance efficiency, engagement, and collaboration among team members.
      • Advanced Insights: utilizing data-driven insights to inform decision-making and drive continuous improvement in digital wellbeing and productivity.
    • Leadership in a Digital Age: guidance for leaders to navigate and excel in the digital age, emphasizing the importance of adaptive leadership styles.
      • Leading Hybrid Teams: strategies for leaders to effectively lead teams in hybrid work environments, ensuring cohesion and productivity.
      • Leading Digital Wellbeing: empowering leaders to champion digital wellbeing initiatives and create a culture that prioritizes employee well-being.
    • Workshops and training programs: If required, a specific proposal can be made for workshops and training programs, based on the data-driven insights and pulses, aimed at educating employees and managers on how to improve digital habits and overall wellbeing.
  • Including:
      • Prioritized actions: clear prioritization of actions to help you focus on the most critical areas that require immediate attention. Actions are categorized based on their potential impact and feasibility.
      • Timeline and milestones: a timeline that defines when each action should be implemented and key milestones to track progress. This ensures a structured approach to achieving your digital wellbeing and productivity goals.
      • Resource allocation: guidance on the allocation of resources, including budget, technology, and personnel, required to implement the actions effectively.

The scope and deliverables of the actions are designed to empower your organization to take tangible steps toward improving digital wellbeing and productivity. This structured approach ensures that you can address specific pain points, enhance overall workplace wellness, and ultimately achieve a more productive and healthier workforce.

Activation of advanced insights reports
