Service Model

Infrastructure management

This service is dependant of other layers which are indicated in the ‘Obligatory’ chapter of this document and which have their own Service Description. The combination of the layers assures the customer of the full stack management.

Service summary

Infrastructure management

The Service provides the tasks and activities needed to manage an Oracle Engineered system environment (The Infrastructure) from a technical perspective. Together with the underlaying obligatory services, Cegeka unburdens the customer with all technical aspects for such platforms.

The Standard Service features are focussed to keep the Service up-and-running, to keep it secure and to optimize its technical parameters.

The Infrastructure

The Oracle Engineered Systems Appliances in scope are:

  • Exadata Appliance (Database platform)
    • Compute Nodes
    • Storage Cell Nodes
    • Switches (ROCE + Admin)
    • Power Distribution Units
    • OS management (Oracle Linux)
  • Private Cloud Appliance (PCA) (Application and Database platform)
    • Compute Nodes
    • Controllers and disk enclosures (internal ZFS)
    • Switches (ROCE + Admin)
    • Power Distribution Units
    • Note: PCA OS management is not included in the service. These are managed through regular OS management services
  • Zettabyte File System Appliance (ZFS) (Storage platform)
    • Controllers
    • Disk Enclosures
    • Switches (TOR)
    • Power Distribution Units
  • Zero Data Loss Recovery Appliance (ZDLRA) (Backup & Recovery platform)
    • Compute Nodes
    • Storage Cell Nodes
    • Switches (ROCE + Admin)
    • Power Distribution Units
    • OS management (Oracle Linux)
  • Oracle Advanced Support Gateway Server (OASG)
    • Either physical Oracle appliance (not included in the service)
    • Either VM to be provisioned by Cegeka according specific Oracle requirements. (*), with additional OS management services

Service features

Standard Service features

Infrastructure knowledge

Cegeka ensures skilled engineers with experience on the Oracle Engineered Systems infrastructure in order to perform the Service. Cegeka works together with Oracle based on the Plantinum Support contract that has been signed between Oracle and the Customer.

Technical setup

Cegeka advises in the setup of the platform in order to set it up according to the technology requirements of the Infrastructure and in line with the best practices of Oracle (MAA). Oracle Maximum Availability Architecture (MAA) consists of a set of best practices blueprints for the integrated use of Oracle’s High Availability (HA) and Disaster Recovery (DR) technologies that ensure this level of availability.

This may be different for each Customer environment (DTAP - Development, Test, Acceptance, Production and it’s variants).

Cegeka advises the Customer aligned to these principles.

Cegeka performs and maintains the technical installation of the Infrastructure on the agreed platform ensuring its correct working, in line with Oracle Platinum support principles.

Technical setup is documented on operational level and is available to the Customer.

Technical management

Cegeka is responsible for the technical operation of the Infrastructure and to assure it’s correct running during the agreed availability windows.

Housekeeping

Recurrent maintenance activities (housekeeping) are needed to ensure the correct working of the Infrastructure. Automation is applied where possible.

Proactive management

Housekeeping includes following regular checks:

  • The technical setup and the running of the Infrastructure
  • Ensure that the Engineered System appliance components (compute nodes, cell nodes, switches, storage performance) are properly monitored following the standard monitoring plan
  • Maintain optimal performance by monitoring and measuring - on an ongoing basis - the key performance indicators
  • Analyse and diagnose the infrastructure to achieve efficient use of capacity to deliver service requirements
  • system trends (trends of CPU/memory/disk usage) on deviations in evolution
  • Maintain optimal versioning of required diagnostic tools (Oracle AHF, exachk, orachk, …)
  • The OS management of the indicated Oracle Linux OS’ses

Resolution actions are triggered when needed and followed to assure the SLA.

Reactive management

  • Troubleshooting of incidents and problems with appliance components (within the managed scope), escalating support calls to vendor when required
  • Execute/assist in hardware replacements for appliance components when hardware failure is determined, and replacement is indicated
  • Execution of changes according to the change management process

Administration and reporting

  • Document all changes via change management process
  • Provide capacity reporting on a monthly basis of the Engineered System (within the managed scope)
  • Provide health check reporting four times a year

Patching

The service includes a maximum of 2 patch cycles (per environment) per year for the managed Infrastructure. Patching is done in accordance to the operational Patch Plan.

Note that Oracle provides four patches per year, but it is advised to limit this to two updates for the engineered systems due to impact on the engineered system and effort required for application testing after upgrades.

The patching is vendor (Oracle) supported, offered and aligned. The Service is responsible for starting up the patching process (ie. involving Oracle Platinum support), arrange all pre-requisites (including planning, running exachecks and providing the results to Oracle), supervise the patching process executed by Oracle and plan and communicate with the Customer. The patching follows the Oracle Platinum provided process.

For the patching it is required to have an active Oracle Platinum support contract for the Engineered System in scope. Cegeka will discuss the patching process proactively with the Customer, e.g. minimum 3 months before patching.

The Admin switches components in each Engineered System fall outside Platinum support contract but will be patched for minor releases within this Service (execution by Cegeka). Major releases are considered upgrades that need to be scheduled and performed separately at an additional fee.

The PDU components in each Engineered System (Power Distribution Units) fall outside Platinum support contract but will be patched for minor releases within this Service (execution by Cegeka). Major releases are considered upgrades that need to be scheduled and performed separately at an additional fee.

The patching only applies to the Engineered System’s firmware and software up to the operating system of the Virtual Machines running on the Engineered Systems, except for PCA where it is limited to the virtualisation layer. Application, Middleware, Database and Grid patching is out of scope of this Service (and covered in the application and database management services). The included Patching only applies to minor release. Major releases (Life Cycle Management) are considered upgrades that need to be scheduled and performed separately at an additional fee.

Life Cycle Management

Cegeka monitors the Technology version of the service and notifies the Customer in accordance with the agreed LCM policy (see cross-functional Services) when used versions are subject to end-of-support or end-of-life. Appropriate actions (updates, replacement or other actions) are planned in accordance with agreed maintenance windows and will be executed on T&M basis in case of major updates. Patches and minor updates are included in the service cost as part of ‘Patching’.

Monitoring

Cegeka monitors the correct working of the Infrastructure.

  • Cegeka has developed a wide range of infrastructure checks to monitor availability of different components and available resource capacity. Most of them are pro-active based on target thresholds while others are reactive. The monitoring checks and associated thresholds are documented in the monitoring plan.
  • All components of the Engineered System are monitored on a 24/7 basis. A combination of Cegeka and Oracle tooling is used to ensure all data is collected to assure optimal operations of the Engineered System.

Monitoring improvements will be done during the lifecycle of the Service.

Monitoring events are evaluated during the Service Window in accordance to the Service Level of the individual environment. Notable events are treated as Incident tickets and are handled according to regular incident management.

Security and Compliance

Management of technical users

The management of technical users (for engineers) is done by Cegeka in agreement with the Customer and in accordance to the Customer IAM policies.

Detection of security incidents

The security management is performed following the Cegeka internal security policies and the contractual Customer security policies.

Security incidents are created in case a security breach is detected and is immediately communicated to the Customer and Cegeka stakeholders.

SIEM

Additional monitoring can be done based on System and/or Application logs, which can be forwarded to a Customer SIEM solution (Security reason) or other solution (compliance reason, e.g. central collection of application logs to oversee the application stack) on request.

Underlaying Platform hardening

Not applicable for this Service.

Backup and Restore

The operational Backup Plan contains the information on frequency and retention of the backups in accordance to the Customer backup policy.

Duration of a restore is dependant of the request (file or full; size of the item(s) to be restored.

Incident and problem management

Besides monitoring incidents (see ‘Monitoring’) also other incidents can be reported by both the Customer and Cegeka. These will be subject to the regular incident management process (See Cross-functionals).

Related to incident management, Cegeka performs also problem management (See Cross-functionals).

Customers responsibility

  • Active licenses and support contracts from the vendor of the Engineered System, including Oracle Platinum support
  • Allow Cegeka technical staff to interact with Oracle Support for registration and follow up of Service Requests.

Requests

Both the Customer and Cegeka can issue a Request for Change (RFC) or a Request for Information (RFI).

There are multiple types of requests:

  • Service Request
    • This is a pre-defined request
    • Some can be requested via Horizon: these are pre-approved and pre-defined requests
    • Service request that is not pre-approved will be executed after approval by the CAB
    • Service Requests are executed as a change ticket and will be delivered within the indicated lead time.
    • The pre-defined execution is optimal automated.
    • This is used for requests that are often needed (multiple times per month).
    • These have a fixed price.
  • Normal Changes :
    • these are other non pre-defined requests
    • they need an evaluation and analysis where Cegeka needs to deliver a proposal and impact analysis to address the change request (with a T&M estimation).
    • A successive review, approval and planning (maintenance window) by a CAB of the proposal is needed prior to execution.
    • These are billed on T&M basis.

The handling of Requests is subject to the regular change management process (see Cross-functionals).

Out of scope

Everything that is not explicitly mentioned in this service description is by default out of scope of this Service.

Specifically:

  • Purchasing and/or leasing of the Infrastructure
  • License purchase and ownership
  • Management of the databases and/or applications running on the engineered system. Ie. these may be part of the respective contracted Services for Database Management or Application Management
  • Consultancy on the use of the platform
  • Management of VMs on top of the PCA engineered systems. Note that these are covered through the service SLA Gold/Silver/Bronze and charged according to the price for that service.

RACI role definition:

  • R-Responsible: The party who is responsible for the execution of the activity.
  • A- Accountable: The party ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the task and/or activity as performed by the Responsible. For each task and/or activity exactly one Accountable must be specified. The Accountable has the mandate to accept/dismiss on work provided by the Responsible. The Accountable is by default reactive.
  • C-Consulted: The party who must be consulted and must approve or provide input prior to execution. This party (partly) gives direction to the result. This is about two-way communication.
  • I-Informed: That is the person/party who is informed about the decisions, progress and results achieved, so that the next step can be taken. This is about one-way communication.
